How DEX aggregation works on Solana

Liquidity on Solana is distributed across Raydium, Orca, Meteora, Phoenix, and dozens of other venues. When you swap directly on any single DEX, your order applies full price impact to that pool alone -- the pool state changes with every trade, and larger orders move the price progressively further from the quote. An aggregator solves this by running your swap across multiple pools simultaneously, routing the full order through the deepest venue or splitting it to reduce impact on each pool individually.

Solana uses concentrated liquidity pool architectures (CLMM on Raydium and Orca, DLMM on Meteora) where depth clusters tightly around the current price and thins rapidly outside a narrow tick range. A trade large enough to exhaust the active range starts filling at progressively worse prices. Splitting the route across Raydium and Orca simultaneously -- or adding Meteora to the mix -- keeps more of the order inside the deep, liquid part of each pool. At $10,000 trade size the difference in tokens received is already measurable. At $50,000 it is significant.

Frequently asked questions

What is a DEX aggregator and why does it get better prices?
A DEX aggregator routes your swap across multiple decentralized exchanges simultaneously rather than executing on a single pool. On Solana, liquidity is fragmented across Raydium, Orca, Meteora, and dozens of other venues. A direct swap on any one DEX applies full price impact to that pool. An aggregator finds the deepest available pool or splits your order across pools to reduce impact -- the result is more tokens received for the same input amount.
Is the swap non-custodial? Do I need to sign up?
Yes -- non-custodial means your private keys and funds stay in your wallet until the swap executes on-chain. There is no account, no sign-up, and no KYC at any point. Connect your wallet, review the quoted price, and confirm the transaction. Your tokens move directly through the smart contract.
What is slippage tolerance and how should I set it?
Slippage tolerance is the maximum price deviation you will accept between the quoted rate and the actual fill price. Setting 1% means you accept any fill up to 1% worse than quoted. On liquid pairs, 0.5% is usually sufficient. On thinner pools or new tokens, you may need 1-2%. Setting it too low causes transactions to fail on volatile pairs; too high exposes you to worse fills when pools move during execution.
What chains are supported?
Solana is the primary chain with full SPL token support across Raydium, Orca, Meteora, Phoenix, and other major Solana AMMs. EVM chains including Ethereum, Base, Arbitrum, BNB Chain, Polygon, and Avalanche are supported. Cross-chain swaps between Solana and EVM networks are handled in a single step -- no separate bridge transaction required.
What is the difference between swapping on Raydium directly versus using a DEX aggregator?
A direct Raydium swap executes at the current CLMM pool price with full price impact applied to your order size. An aggregator runs the same trade simultaneously across Raydium, Orca, Meteora, and other active pools, then either routes the full order through the deepest pool or splits it to reduce impact. On Solana's concentrated liquidity architecture, where depth clusters tightly around the current price, the difference in execution cost is measurable on trades above $5,000 and significant on trades above $25,000.
