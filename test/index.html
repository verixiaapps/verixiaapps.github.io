Amazon Verification Security Check Scam Explained

If you received an Amazon verification or security alert message, it may look legitimate at first glance. These messages often claim there is an issue with your account, a suspicious login, or a payment problem.

Scammers use urgency and fear to push you into clicking a link or entering your login details. Many of these messages are designed to look identical to real Amazon emails or texts.

Common Warning Signs

  • Urgent language like "verify immediately" or "account will be locked"
  • Links that do not lead to official Amazon domains
  • Requests for passwords, codes, or payment details
  • Unusual sender email addresses

What To Do

Do not click links or respond directly. Instead, go to Amazon through the official app or website and check your account notifications.

If something feels off, it is safer to assume it may be a scam and verify independently.
