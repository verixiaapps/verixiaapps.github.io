About report scam message

Scam messages often appear as urgent requests for money, personal information, or login details. They may claim you’ve won a prize, face a problem with your account, or need to confirm sensitive data. Common warning signs include poor spelling, suspicious links, sender addresses that don’t match official contacts, and pressure to act quickly. If you receive such a message, do not respond or click any links. Instead, verify the information through official channels like the company's website or customer service. Report the message to your email provider, phone carrier, or relevant authorities to help prevent others from falling victim. Staying cautious helps protect your personal information and finances.

Common Scam Warning Signs

  • Requests for urgent payment or cryptocurrency
  • Messages impersonating companies or government agencies
  • Links directing you to unfamiliar websites
  • Requests for passwords, codes or personal information
  • Offers that appear too good to be true
