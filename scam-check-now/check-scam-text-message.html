Common Text Message Scams

• Fake delivery notifications (USPS, FedEx, UPS)
• Bank or payment verification messages
• Job offers sent through text
• Cryptocurrency investment scams
• Fake account security alerts

How the Scam Message Checker Works

Paste the text message you received. The system scans the message for common scam patterns including urgency, impersonation, suspicious links, payment requests, and known phishing tactics.

The result will show a risk level such as Low Risk, Medium Risk, or High Risk, along with explanations for why the message may be dangerous.

Warning Signs of an SMS Scam

• The message creates urgency or fear
• A link asks you to log in or verify information
• You are asked to send money or gift cards
• The sender claims to be a bank or company unexpectedly
• The message contains shortened or suspicious links
