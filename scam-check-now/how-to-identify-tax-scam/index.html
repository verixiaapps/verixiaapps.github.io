About how to identify tax scam

Tax-related scams often involve fake calls, emails, or messages claiming you owe money or that there are issues with your returns. Scammers may pressure you to pay immediately, use threatening language, or ask for personal details like Social Security numbers or bank info. Common warning signs include unsolicited contact, requests for payment via unusual methods (like gift cards or wire transfers), and poor grammar or spelling. If you receive such communication, don’t respond or share personal information. Instead, verify by contacting the official tax agency directly using trusted phone numbers or websites. Reporting the suspicious contact to authorities can help protect others from falling victim. Stay cautious and always confirm before taking action.
