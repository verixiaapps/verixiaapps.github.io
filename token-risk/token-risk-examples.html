Token Risk Examples (Plain English)

These examples show how token risk appears in real situations. They are structural examples — not endorsements or warnings.

No prices. No predictions. Just how risk typically shows up.

Example: High token risk

This is high risk.

A new token launches with a small team controlling the supply.

  • Contracts can be upgraded
  • Liquidity is added manually
  • Rules can change quickly

Everything may look fine — until control is exercised.

Example: Medium token risk

This is medium risk.

A token is widely used but depends on governance or infrastructure.

  • Votes determine future changes
  • Bridges or custodians are involved
  • Upgrades are possible but slow

Risk exists, but changes are usually visible in advance.

Example: Low token risk

This is low risk.

A long-established token with simple mechanics.

  • No central controller
  • No upgrade paths
  • Few dependencies

Risk still exists — but there are fewer failure points.

Why examples matter

Most people understand risk faster through examples than definitions.

Once you see the structure, you can recognize it anywhere.
