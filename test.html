Why Token Risk Exists

Some tokens look active long before they look safe. Hype, fast volume, and social momentum can hide weak liquidity, risky ownership, concentrated wallets, or launch conditions that punish late buyers. This page is built to surface those signals early.

L Liquidity signals

Thin liquidity can turn a normal-looking entry into heavy slippage, trapped exits, or unstable price action fast.

C Contract signals

Permission risk, ownership concerns, and unclear controls matter when the downside of trust is irreversible.

H Holder concentration

A token can feel strong until a few wallets control too much supply, liquidity pressure, or exit power.

R Launch risk

Fresh launches, sudden spikes, and rushed entries can create the most expensive mistakes when context is weak.
