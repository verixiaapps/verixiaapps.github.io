Scams involving Ethereum often promise quick profits or free coins in exchange for sending a small amount first. They may appear as messages on social media, emails, or fake websites pretending to be official platforms. Common warning signs include requests for upfront payments, offers that sound too good to be true, urgent deadlines, and suspicious links. Legitimate services never ask you to send money to receive funds back. If you receive such a message, do not respond or send any cryptocurrency. Instead, verify the information through official sources, report the scam to the platform where you found it, and consider blocking the sender to protect yourself from potential fraud.
