Social Security scams often involve fake calls or messages claiming there’s a problem with your benefits or account. Scammers may demand personal information, threaten legal action, or ask for payment. Common warning signs include urgent requests, unfamiliar phone numbers, poor grammar, or callers refusing to provide official contact details. If you receive such a message, do not share any personal information. Instead, hang up and contact the Social Security Administration directly using their official website or phone number. Reporting suspicious activity helps protect you and others from fraud. Always stay cautious and verify requests before responding.
