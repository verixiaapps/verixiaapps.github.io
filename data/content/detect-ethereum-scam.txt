Scams involving Ethereum often promise huge profits, asking you to send cryptocurrency or private keys. They may mimic official platforms or use urgent messages to pressure you. Common warning signs include unsolicited offers, requests for personal information, unrealistic returns, and poor spelling or grammar. If you receive such messages, never share your private keys or send money. Instead, verify the source independently through official websites or trusted contacts. Report suspicious activity to platform support or relevant authorities to help protect yourself and others. Staying cautious and informed is key to avoiding these fraudulent schemes.
