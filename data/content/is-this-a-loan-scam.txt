Scams offering loans often promise easy approval with little or no credit check. They may ask for upfront fees, personal information, or payment before providing funds. Common warning signs include requests for advance payments, pressure to act quickly, unclear contract terms, or unsolicited offers via email or social media. If you receive such an offer, do not share your personal or financial details. Verify the lender’s legitimacy through official websites or financial authorities. Report suspicious activity to consumer protection agencies and consider consulting a trusted financial advisor before proceeding. Staying cautious helps protect your money and personal information from fraud.
