Investment scams often promise high returns with little risk, using convincing offers or pressure to act quickly. They may come as phone calls, emails, or social media messages claiming to be from trustworthy companies or experts. Common warning signs include unrealistic profits, requests for personal information or upfront fees, lack of clear details, and pushy sales tactics. If you receive such an offer, don’t respond immediately. Research the company thoroughly through official sources, check for reviews or complaints, and consult a trusted financial advisor. Report suspicious activity to local authorities or consumer protection agencies to help protect yourself and others from fraud. Staying cautious can save you from losing money and personal data.
