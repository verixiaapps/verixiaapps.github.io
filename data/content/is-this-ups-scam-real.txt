Many scammers pretend to be from UPS to trick people into giving personal information or money. These scams often involve fake emails, texts, or calls saying there’s a problem with a package, like unpaid fees or delivery issues. Common warning signs include urgent language, requests for payment via unusual methods (like gift cards), suspicious links, or emails from unverified addresses. If you receive such a message, don’t click links or provide personal details. Instead, check your package status directly on the official UPS website or contact UPS customer service using verified contact information. Staying cautious can help protect you from falling victim to these scams.
