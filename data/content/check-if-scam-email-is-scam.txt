Scam emails often look urgent or too good to be true, claiming you won a prize or need to verify personal information. Common warning signs include poor spelling, unfamiliar sender addresses, unexpected attachments, and requests for sensitive data like passwords or money. They may also use threatening language to rush you into action. If you receive a suspicious message, do not click on links or download attachments. Instead, verify the sender by contacting the organization directly using trusted contact details. Delete the email if unsure, and consider reporting it to your email provider or relevant authorities to help protect yourself and others.
