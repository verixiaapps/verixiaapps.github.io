Gift card scams often involve someone asking you to pay with gift cards for fake emergencies, prizes, or debts. Scammers may claim to be from a trusted company, government agency, or family member in trouble. Common warning signs include urgent requests to buy gift cards, pressure to keep it secret, or demands to share card numbers and PINs immediately. If you receive such a request, don’t send any gift card information. Instead, verify the situation by contacting the company or person directly using official contact details. Report suspicious messages to the gift card provider and local authorities to help prevent others from being tricked. Always stay cautious and remember that legitimate organizations don’t ask for payment this way.
