Scam messages often appear urgent, asking for personal information, passwords, or money. They may claim you've won a prize, face a problem with your account, or need to verify details quickly. Common warning signs include poor grammar, unfamiliar sender addresses, suspicious links, and pressure to act immediately. If you get such a message, avoid clicking any links or sharing information. Instead, verify the claim by contacting the company or person directly using trusted contact details. Reporting the message to relevant authorities or your service provider can help protect others. Staying cautious and double-checking unexpected requests keeps your information safe.
