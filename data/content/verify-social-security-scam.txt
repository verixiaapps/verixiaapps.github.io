Scammers often pretend to be from Social Security to steal personal information or money. They may call, email, or send letters claiming your number is at risk, or that you owe money. Common warning signs include urgent demands for payment, requests for personal details like your Social Security number or bank info, and threats of arrest. Genuine agencies will not ask for sensitive information through these methods or demand immediate payment. If you receive such contact, do not share any details or send money. Instead, hang up or delete the message, and report it to the official Social Security Administration website or the Federal Trade Commission. Staying cautious helps protect your identity and finances.
