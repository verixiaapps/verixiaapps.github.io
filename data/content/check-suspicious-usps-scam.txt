Scammers often pose as postal service employees, sending fake emails, texts, or calls about undelivered packages or fees. These messages may ask for personal information, payment, or prompt you to click suspicious links. Common warning signs include poor spelling, urgent demands, unfamiliar sender addresses, and unexpected requests for money or details. If you receive such a message, do not respond or click any links. Instead, verify by contacting the official postal service directly using their website or phone number. Avoid sharing personal or financial information and report the suspicious communication to the postal authority or a consumer protection agency to help prevent fraud.
