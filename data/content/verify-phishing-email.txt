Phishing scams often appear as urgent emails claiming to be from trusted companies, asking you to verify personal info or reset passwords. Common warning signs include poor spelling, unexpected requests, suspicious links, and generic greetings like "Dear Customer." The sender’s email address may look unusual or mimic a real one closely. If you receive such a message, avoid clicking links or downloading attachments. Instead, contact the company directly using official contact details to confirm the email’s legitimacy. Always keep your software updated and use security tools to stay protected. Staying alert can help you avoid falling victim to these deceptive tactics.
