Scams targeting Chase Bank customers often involve fake emails, calls, or texts pretending to be from the bank. They may ask for personal information, passwords, or request urgent payments. Common warning signs include poor spelling, generic greetings, unexpected messages, and threats of account suspension. If you receive a suspicious message, do not click any links or share details. Instead, contact Chase directly using official phone numbers or the bank’s website to verify the communication. Reporting the incident helps protect others and can prevent fraud. Always stay cautious and keep your account information secure.
