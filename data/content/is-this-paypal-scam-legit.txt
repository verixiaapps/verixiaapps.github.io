Scams pretending to be from PayPal often involve fake emails or messages claiming there’s a problem with your account or a suspicious transaction. These messages may ask you to click links or provide personal information. Common warning signs include poor spelling and grammar, urgent language pressuring you to act quickly, and URLs that don’t match the official site. The sender’s email address may also look suspicious. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or provide information. Instead, log in directly to your official PayPal account through the website or app to check for issues. You can also report the message to PayPal’s fraud department to help protect yourself and others.
