Scams related to Ethereum often involve fake offers promising high returns, free tokens, or urgent requests to send crypto for “verification.” Scammers may impersonate trusted figures or services to gain trust. Common warning signs include unsolicited messages, requests for private keys or passwords, unrealistic promises, and links to suspicious websites. If you receive such an approach, do not share personal information or send funds. Always double-check with official sources and use trusted platforms. Report the suspicious activity to relevant authorities or the platform involved to help protect yourself and others. Stay cautious and verify before taking any action involving your digital assets.
