Phishing emails often pretend to be from trusted sources, like banks or popular websites, asking you to click a link or provide personal info. They usually create a sense of urgency, such as warning about account problems or winning a prize. Common warning signs include poor spelling, unusual sender addresses, unexpected requests, and suspicious links. If you get a message like this, don’t click any links or download attachments. Instead, verify by contacting the company directly using official contact details. Reporting the message to your email provider or IT department can help protect others. Staying cautious helps keep your information safe.
