Scammers often impersonate Chase Bank by sending fake emails, texts, or calls claiming there’s an issue with your account. These messages may ask for personal information, passwords, or prompt you to click suspicious links. Common warning signs include poor spelling and grammar, urgent demands for action, unfamiliar sender addresses, and requests for sensitive details. Legitimate communications will never ask for your full password or PIN. If you receive a suspicious message, do not reply or click any links. Instead, contact Chase directly using the official website or phone number to verify the message. Reporting the scam to the bank helps protect others from fraud. Staying cautious can keep your information safe.
