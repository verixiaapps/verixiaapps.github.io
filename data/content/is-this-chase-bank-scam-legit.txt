Scammers often impersonate Chase Bank by sending fake emails, texts, or calls claiming there’s an issue with your account. These messages may ask for personal details, passwords, or direct you to a fake website. Common warning signs include spelling mistakes, urgent language, unknown links, and requests for sensitive information. Legitimate banks rarely ask for personal info via messages. If you receive such communication, do not click any links or give out information. Instead, contact Chase Bank directly using official phone numbers or their website to verify the message's authenticity. Reporting suspicious activity helps protect you and others from fraud.
