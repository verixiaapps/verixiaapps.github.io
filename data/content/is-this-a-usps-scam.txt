Scammers often send fake messages pretending to be from the postal service, claiming there’s a package issue or unpaid fees. These fake alerts usually ask for personal information or payment. Common warning signs include poor spelling or grammar, urgent demands, unknown links, or requests for sensitive details. Legitimate communications will never ask for payment via unusual methods or threaten immediate action. If you get a suspicious message, don’t click any links or provide information. Instead, visit the official postal service website or contact their customer support directly to verify. Reporting the incident to the postal inspector or your local authorities can also help protect others. Stay cautious to avoid falling for these tricks.
