Scammers often send fake messages pretending to be from Chase Bank, trying to steal your personal information or money. These messages may claim there’s a problem with your account or ask you to verify your details through a suspicious link. Common warning signs include spelling mistakes, urgent language, unfamiliar sender addresses, and requests for sensitive data. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or provide personal info. Instead, contact Chase Bank directly using official phone numbers or their website to verify the message’s authenticity. Always stay cautious to protect your financial information and avoid falling victim to these scams.
