Remote job scams often promise easy work-from-home opportunities with high pay and little effort. They may ask for upfront fees, personal information, or require you to buy training materials. Common warning signs include vague job descriptions, requests for money, poor grammar in messages, and unsolicited offers.

If you receive such an offer, research the company thoroughly, check reviews, and never pay to apply or start working. Avoid sharing sensitive details like your Social Security number early in the process. When in doubt, report suspicious messages to job platforms or consumer protection agencies. Staying cautious helps protect your personal information and prevents financial loss.
