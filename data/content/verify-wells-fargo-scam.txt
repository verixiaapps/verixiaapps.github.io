Scammers pretending to be from Wells Fargo often call or email, claiming there’s an issue with your account. They may ask for personal information, passwords, or request you to send money. Common warning signs include urgent or threatening language, unfamiliar phone numbers, requests for sensitive details, and poor grammar. Official communications typically do not ask for your full password or a PIN. If you receive such a message, do not share any personal information. Instead, contact Wells Fargo directly using the phone number on their official website. Reporting suspicious activity helps protect you and others from fraud. Always stay cautious and verify before responding.
