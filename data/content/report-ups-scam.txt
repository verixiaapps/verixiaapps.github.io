A common scam involves fake messages claiming to be from a popular delivery company, saying there's a problem with a package or a missed delivery. These messages often include a link or ask for personal information. Warning signs include poor grammar, urgent language, unknown sender addresses, and unexpected requests for payment or details. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or provide personal info. Instead, verify directly with the official delivery company using their website or customer service number. Reporting the scam to the company and local authorities helps protect others from falling victim. Always be cautious with unexpected communications about deliveries.
