Remote job scams often promise easy work-from-home opportunities with high pay but ask for personal information or upfront fees. These fraudulent offers may come via email or social media and include vague job descriptions or pressure to act quickly. Common warning signs include requests for money, suspicious email addresses, poor grammar, and promises that sound too good to be true. If you receive such an offer, avoid sharing personal details or sending money. Instead, research the company, verify job postings on official sites, and report suspicious contacts to relevant authorities to protect yourself from fraud.
