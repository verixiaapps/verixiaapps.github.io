Scam messages often pretend to be from banks or trusted companies, asking you to verify personal information or claim you've won money. They may include urgent language to pressure you into quick action. Common warning signs include spelling mistakes, suspicious links, unfamiliar sender addresses, and requests for sensitive data like passwords or social security numbers. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or provide information. Instead, delete it and contact the company directly using official contact details to confirm. Reporting the message to your email provider or local authorities can help protect others from falling victim to similar fraud. Staying cautious helps keep your personal information safe.
