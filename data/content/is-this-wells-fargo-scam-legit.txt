Scammers often impersonate Wells Fargo by sending fake emails or texts claiming urgent account issues. These messages may ask for personal details, passwords, or payment to "resolve" a problem. Common warning signs include poor spelling, generic greetings like “Dear Customer,” unexpected requests for sensitive information, and links directing to suspicious websites. Legitimate banks don’t ask for private details via email or text. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or provide information. Instead, contact Wells Fargo directly using official phone numbers or website addresses to verify the alert. Reporting the scam to the bank and the Federal Trade Commission can help protect others from falling victim.
