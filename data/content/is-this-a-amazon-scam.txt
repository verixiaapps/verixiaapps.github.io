Many scams pretend to be from Amazon to trick people. They often come as emails or texts saying there's a problem with your order or account. These messages may ask for personal information, payment details, or urge you to click a suspicious link. Common warning signs include poor spelling and grammar, generic greetings like "Dear Customer," urgent language pressuring quick action, and unfamiliar sender addresses. If you receive such a message, don’t click any links or provide information. Instead, verify the claim by logging directly into your official account through the official website or app. You can also contact Amazon customer service to confirm if there’s an issue. Reporting suspicious messages helps protect others from fraud.
