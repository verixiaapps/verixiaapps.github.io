Scammers often impersonate FedEx by sending fake emails or messages claiming there is a delivery issue or unpaid fee. These messages may ask for personal information or payment to release a package. Common warning signs include poor spelling, urgent language, suspicious links, or requests for sensitive details. Official FedEx communications rarely ask for payment via unusual methods like gift cards or wire transfers. If you receive such a message, do not click links or provide personal info. Instead, contact FedEx directly through their official website or customer service number to confirm any delivery concerns. Always stay cautious to protect yourself from fraud.
