Tax scams often involve fake calls, emails, or messages claiming you owe money or face legal trouble. Scammers may threaten arrest, demand immediate payment, or ask for personal information. Common warning signs include unsolicited contact, requests for payment via unusual methods like gift cards or wire transfers, and aggressive or threatening language. Genuine tax authorities usually send official letters first and do not demand instant payment over the phone. If you get such a message, do not provide personal details or send money. Instead, contact your local tax agency directly using official contact information to verify the claim. Reporting the suspicious activity to relevant authorities can help protect others from falling victim.
