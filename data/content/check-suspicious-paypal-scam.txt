Scammers often send fake messages pretending to be from PayPal, claiming there’s a problem with your account or a suspicious transaction. These messages usually ask you to click a link and enter personal or financial details. Common warning signs include poor grammar, urgent language, unfamiliar sender addresses, and links that don’t lead to the official site. If you receive such a message, don’t click any links or provide information. Instead, log in directly to your account through the official website or app to check for issues. You can also forward suspicious emails to PayPal’s fraud team at spoof@paypal.com and delete the message to stay safe.
