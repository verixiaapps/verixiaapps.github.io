A common scam involves someone requesting money through a popular payment app, often pretending to be a friend, family member, or trusted business. The message may ask for urgent help or offer deals that seem too good to be true. Warning signs include pressure to send funds quickly, requests for payment outside normal channels, or unfamiliar contacts asking for money. If you receive such a message, do not send funds immediately. Instead, verify the request by contacting the person directly using a known phone number or email. Report suspicious activity to your bank or the app’s support team to protect yourself and others. Staying cautious helps prevent financial loss.
