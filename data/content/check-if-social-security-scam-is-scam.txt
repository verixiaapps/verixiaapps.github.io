Social Security scams often involve calls, messages, or emails claiming there’s a problem with your benefits or account. Scammers may threaten arrest or demand personal information like your Social Security number or bank details. Common warning signs include urgent threats, requests for payment via gift cards or wire transfer, and unfamiliar phone numbers. Official agencies never ask for sensitive details this way or demand immediate payment. If you receive such contact, do not share personal information. Instead, hang up and verify by contacting the Social Security Administration directly using official phone numbers or their website. Reporting the incident to local authorities and the FTC can also help protect others from fraud.
