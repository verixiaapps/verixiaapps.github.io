Remote job scams often promise easy work and high pay but ask for personal information or upfront fees. They may claim you need to pay for training, equipment, or background checks before starting. Common warning signs include unsolicited offers, vague job descriptions, requests for money, or communication only through personal email or messaging apps. If you receive such an offer, do not share personal or financial details. Research the company online and check reviews from trusted sources. Report suspicious messages to job platforms or authorities to help protect others. Always trust your instincts—legitimate employers won’t pressure you to pay before you work.
