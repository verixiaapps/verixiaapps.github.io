Scams involving Ethereum often promise quick profits or free coins in exchange for sending cryptocurrency first. They may appear as messages from fake accounts, phishing websites, or investment offers that seem too good to be true. Common warning signs include unsolicited requests for personal info or private keys, pressure to act fast, and deals promising guaranteed returns. If you encounter such offers, do not share your wallet details or send any funds. Verify the source independently and report suspicious activity to relevant platforms. Staying cautious and double-checking information can help protect your assets from fraud.
