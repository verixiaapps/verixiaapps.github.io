Scams involving PayPal often appear as fake emails or messages claiming there's an issue with your account or a payment problem. They may ask you to click a link or provide personal information. Common warning signs include poor spelling, urgent language, unfamiliar sender addresses, and links that don’t lead to the official site. If you receive a suspicious message, don’t click any links or share details. Instead, log in directly to your account through the official website or app to check for issues. You can also report the message to PayPal’s fraud department. Staying cautious helps protect your personal and financial information from fraud.
