Investment scams often promise high returns with little risk, using flashy offers or urgent calls to act fast. Common warning signs include unsolicited messages, pressure to invest quickly, requests for personal or financial information, and guarantees of large profits. If you receive such an offer, do not share any details or send money. Instead, verify the company's credentials through official financial authorities. Report the suspicious activity to your local consumer protection agency or financial regulator to help prevent others from falling victim. Staying cautious and informed is key to avoiding fraud.
