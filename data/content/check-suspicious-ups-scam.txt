A common scam involves fake messages claiming to be from UPS about a missed delivery or a package issue. These scams often ask you to click a link, provide personal information, or pay fees to release a parcel. Warning signs include unexpected emails or texts, poor spelling or grammar, urgent language, and unfamiliar sender addresses. Genuine companies usually don’t request payment or sensitive details this way. If you receive such a message, don’t click any links or share information. Instead, verify the claim by contacting UPS directly through their official website or customer service. Reporting the suspicious message helps protect others from falling victim. Stay cautious and double-check before taking action.
