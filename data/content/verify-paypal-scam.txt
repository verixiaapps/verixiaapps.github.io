Scammers often send fake emails or messages pretending to be from PayPal, claiming there’s a problem with your account or a suspicious payment. These messages usually ask you to click a link and enter personal details. Common warning signs include poor spelling, urgent language, unexpected requests for sensitive information, and email addresses that don’t match official PayPal contacts. If you receive such a message, don’t click any links or provide information. Instead, log in to your account directly through the official website or app to check for issues. You can also forward suspicious emails to PayPal’s fraud department and delete the message to stay safe.
