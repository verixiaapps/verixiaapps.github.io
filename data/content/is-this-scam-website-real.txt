Scam websites often mimic legitimate sites to trick people into sharing personal or financial information. They may offer deals that seem too good to be true or ask for payment upfront. Common warning signs include poor design, spelling errors, suspicious URLs, and requests for sensitive data. If you encounter such a site, do not enter any information or make payments. Instead, verify the website’s authenticity by checking reviews or official sources. Report the site to authorities or consumer protection agencies to help prevent others from falling victim. Always use caution when online offers seem unusual or urgent.
