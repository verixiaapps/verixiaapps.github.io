A fake job scam often involves offers that seem too good to be true, such as high salaries for little work or quick hiring without interviews. Scammers may ask for personal information, upfront fees, or money to buy equipment. Common warning signs include poor grammar, unofficial email addresses, vague job descriptions, and pressure to act quickly. If you receive such an offer, avoid sharing personal details or sending money. Instead, research the company independently and contact them directly using official contact information. Reporting suspicious messages to job platforms or local authorities can help protect others. Always trust your instincts and verify before committing.
