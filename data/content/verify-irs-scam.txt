Scammers pretending to be the IRS often call or email, claiming you owe taxes or face legal trouble. They pressure you to pay immediately using gift cards, wire transfers, or prepaid debit cards. Common warning signs include threats of arrest, demanding secrecy, or requests for unusual payment methods. The IRS will never call demanding immediate payment or ask for personal information by email. If you receive a suspicious contact, do not provide personal details. Instead, hang up and contact the IRS directly using official phone numbers or visit their website. Reporting the incident to the Treasury Inspector General for Tax Administration helps protect others from fraud. Stay cautious and verify requests through trusted sources.
