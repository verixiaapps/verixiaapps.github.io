Scams involving cryptocurrency often promise quick profits or claim to be official offers. They may ask for personal information, upfront payments, or private keys. Common warning signs include unsolicited messages, pressure to act fast, offers that seem too good to be true, and requests to send money to unknown addresses. If you encounter such messages, do not share personal details or send funds. Instead, verify the source through official channels and report the incident to relevant authorities or platforms. Staying cautious helps protect your assets and personal data from fraud.
