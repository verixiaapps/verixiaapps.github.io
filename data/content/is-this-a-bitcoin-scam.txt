Scams involving digital currency often promise quick, easy profits or ask for personal information. They may appear as messages claiming you’ve won money, requests to send funds first, or offers that sound too good to be true. Common warning signs include unsolicited contact, urgent demands for payment, and requests for private keys or passwords. If you receive such an offer, be cautious—never share sensitive details or send money to unknown people. Instead, verify the source through trusted channels and report suspicious activity to relevant authorities. Protecting yourself by staying informed and skeptical helps avoid falling victim to these fraudulent schemes.
