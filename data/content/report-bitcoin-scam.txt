Bitcoin scams often involve fake investment offers, urgent requests for payment, or promises of guaranteed returns. Scammers may impersonate trusted figures or companies to gain your trust. Common warning signs include unsolicited messages, pressure to act quickly, requests for private keys or personal information, and deals that seem too good to be true. If you receive such a message, do not send any money or share personal details. Instead, verify the sender’s identity independently, report the suspicious activity to relevant authorities or platforms, and warn friends or family to help prevent others from falling victim. Staying cautious can protect you from financial loss and fraud.
