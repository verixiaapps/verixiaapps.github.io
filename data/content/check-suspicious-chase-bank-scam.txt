Scammers impersonating Chase Bank often send fake emails, texts, or calls claiming there’s a problem with your account. They may ask for personal information, passwords, or payment details. Common warning signs include poor spelling, urgent messages demanding immediate action, unknown links, or requests for sensitive data. Legitimate messages from Chase will never ask for your full password or PIN. If you receive a suspicious message, do not click any links or provide information. Instead, contact Chase directly using the phone number on their official website or your bank statement to verify the request. Reporting the scam to the bank and relevant authorities can help protect others from fraud.
