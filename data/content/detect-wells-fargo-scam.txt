Scammers often impersonate Wells Fargo to trick people into giving personal information. These messages may look like official emails, texts, or phone calls claiming there's an issue with your account or a suspicious transaction. Common warning signs include poor grammar, urgent requests for personal details, unfamiliar links, or pressure to act quickly. They may ask for passwords, Social Security numbers, or payment information. If you receive such a message, do not respond or click any links. Instead, contact Wells Fargo directly using the phone number on their official website or your bank statement to verify the claim. Always stay cautious and protect your information to avoid falling victim to fraud.
