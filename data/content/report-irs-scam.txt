IRS scams often involve fake calls, emails, or messages claiming you owe taxes or face arrest. Scammers may use threats, demand immediate payment, or request personal information. Common warning signs include aggressive behavior, requests for payment via unusual methods (like gift cards or wire transfers), and official-sounding but suspicious caller IDs. The IRS usually contacts taxpayers by mail first and never demands payment without giving a chance to question the amount. If you receive such contact, do not provide personal details or money. Instead, hang up or delete the message and report it directly to the IRS through their official website or phone number to help protect yourself and others from fraud.
