Scam messages often claim urgent problems, like unpaid bills or account issues, trying to create panic. They may ask for personal info, passwords, or money. Common warning signs include poor grammar, unfamiliar sender numbers, suspicious links, and requests for immediate action. Legitimate companies usually don’t ask for sensitive details via text. If you receive such a message, avoid clicking links or replying. Instead, verify by contacting the company directly using official contact information. Report the message to your mobile provider or local authorities to help protect others. Staying cautious can prevent fraud and keep your information safe.
