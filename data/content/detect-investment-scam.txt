Investment scams often promise high returns with little or no risk, appearing as urgent offers or exclusive opportunities. They may come through emails, phone calls, or social media. Common warning signs include unrealistic profits, pressure to act quickly, requests for personal information or upfront payments, and lack of clear details about the company. If you encounter a suspicious offer, do not share your personal or financial information. Instead, research the company thoroughly and check with trusted sources like financial regulators. Reporting the situation to authorities can help protect others. Always take time to verify before investing money.
