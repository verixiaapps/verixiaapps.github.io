Scammers often pose as UPS or other delivery services to trick people into giving personal information or money. These messages may claim there's a problem with a package, asking you to click a link or provide details to fix it. Common warning signs include urgent language, unexpected requests for payment or personal data, and suspicious links or email addresses. Legitimate companies usually don’t ask for sensitive information via email or text. If you receive such a message, don’t click any links or provide details. Instead, contact UPS directly through their official website or customer service number to verify the claim. Always be cautious to protect your information from fraud.
