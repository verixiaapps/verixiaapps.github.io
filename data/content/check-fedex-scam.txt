Scammers often send fake messages claiming you have a package from a well-known delivery company, asking for personal information or payment. These messages may look official, with logos and tracking numbers, but usually contain spelling errors or unusual links. Common warning signs include urgent requests, unexpected charges, or being asked to provide sensitive details. If you receive such a message, do not click on any links or share personal information. Instead, verify the delivery status directly on the official company website or contact their customer service. Reporting suspicious messages helps protect others from falling victim to fraud.
