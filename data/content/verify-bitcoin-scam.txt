Bitcoin scams often involve fake investment offers, unrealistic promises of high returns, or urgent messages claiming you need to act quickly. Common warning signs include requests for personal information, upfront payments, or pressure to send funds immediately. Scammers may impersonate trusted figures or use fake websites to appear legitimate. If you receive such a message, do not share personal details or send money. Instead, verify the source independently, report the incident to relevant authorities, and seek advice from trusted experts. Staying cautious and informed helps protect your digital assets from fraud.
