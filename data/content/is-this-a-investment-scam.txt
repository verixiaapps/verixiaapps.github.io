Investment scams often promise high returns with little or no risk, urging quick decisions. They may come via emails, phone calls, or social media offers claiming insider tips or guaranteed profits. Common warning signs include unsolicited contact, pressure to act fast, requests for personal or financial information, and vague or overly complex explanations. If you receive such an offer, do not share sensitive details or send money. Instead, research the company thoroughly, consult trusted financial advisors, and report suspicious activity to consumer protection agencies. Staying cautious helps protect your money and personal information from fraud.
