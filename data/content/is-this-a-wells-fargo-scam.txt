This scam often involves fake calls, emails, or texts claiming to be from Wells Fargo, warning about suspicious account activity or urgent security issues. Scammers try to steal personal information, like passwords or Social Security numbers. Common warning signs include poor grammar, urgent requests, unsolicited contact, and links or attachments asking for sensitive data. Official messages usually don’t pressure you to act immediately or ask for personal details via email or phone. If you receive such communication, do not click links or provide information. Instead, contact Wells Fargo directly using a verified phone number or their official website to confirm if the message is real. Staying cautious helps protect your accounts from fraud.
