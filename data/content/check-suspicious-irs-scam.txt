IRS scams often involve fake calls or emails claiming you owe money or face legal trouble. Scammers might threaten arrest, demand immediate payment, or ask for personal info. Common warning signs include urgent threats, requests for payment via gift cards or wire transfer, and caller ID spoofing to appear official. The IRS usually contacts by mail first and never demands payment this way. If you get a suspicious message, do not share personal details or send money. Instead, verify by contacting the IRS directly using official numbers found on their website. Reporting the incident to the Treasury Inspector General for Tax Administration helps protect others from fraud. Stay cautious and protect your personal information.
