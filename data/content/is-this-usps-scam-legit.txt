Scams pretending to be from USPS often involve fake delivery notices claiming a package is held or undelivered. These messages may ask for personal information, payment, or prompt you to click suspicious links. Common warning signs include poor spelling and grammar, urgent language, unknown sender addresses, and requests for sensitive data or money. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or provide information. Instead, verify the delivery status directly on the official USPS website or contact their customer service. Reporting the scam to the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) or local authorities can help protect others from falling victim. Staying cautious helps keep your personal information safe.
