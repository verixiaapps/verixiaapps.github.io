Scammers on Snapchat often send messages pretending to be friends or popular accounts, asking for personal info, money, or to click suspicious links. Common warning signs include urgent requests, unfamiliar links, poor spelling, and offers that seem too good to be true. If you receive a suspicious message, don’t reply or click any links. Instead, block and report the sender using Snapchat’s tools. Always verify with the person directly through another method if you’re unsure. Protect your account by using strong passwords and enabling two-factor authentication. Staying cautious helps keep your information safe from fraud.
