Loan scams often promise quick approval and low-interest rates but require upfront fees or personal information. They may come through emails, texts, or phone calls from unknown sources. Common warning signs include requests for payment before approval, pressure to act fast, offers that seem too good to be true, and lack of clear contact details. If you receive such an offer, avoid sharing personal or financial information. Instead, verify the lender’s legitimacy through official websites or trusted financial institutions. Report suspicious activity to consumer protection agencies to help prevent others from falling victim. Always approach loan offers with caution to protect your finances and identity.
