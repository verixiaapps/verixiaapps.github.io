Scams on TikTok often appear as messages or videos promising easy money, free gifts, or exclusive deals. They may ask you to click links, share personal details, or send money. Common warning signs include offers that seem too good to be true, urgent requests, poor spelling or grammar, and unknown senders. If you receive a suspicious message, avoid clicking any links or sharing your information. Report the user or content to TikTok through the app’s reporting tools. It’s best to block the sender and warn friends to stay safe. Always be cautious and verify offers independently before taking any action.
