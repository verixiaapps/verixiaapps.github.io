Investment scams often promise high returns with little risk and use urgent language to pressure quick decisions. They may come through unsolicited calls, emails, or social media messages and offer unusual opportunities or guaranteed profits. Warning signs include promises that seem too good to be true, lack of clear information about the company, unlicensed sellers, and requests for personal or payment details upfront. If you encounter such an offer, do not share personal data or send money. Instead, research the company through official financial authorities, check for reviews, and seek advice from trusted sources. Reporting suspicious activity to consumer protection agencies can help prevent others from falling victim. Always take time to verify before investing.
