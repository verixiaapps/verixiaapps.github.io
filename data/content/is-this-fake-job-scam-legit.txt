Fake job scams often promise easy work and high pay with little effort. They may ask for personal information, upfront fees, or bank details early on. Common warning signs include unsolicited job offers, vague job descriptions, requests for payment, and poor grammar or spelling in messages. Scammers may also push for quick decisions or use unofficial communication channels. If you receive such an offer, do not share personal or financial information. Research the company independently and contact them directly to verify the job’s authenticity. Report suspicious offers to job sites or local authorities to help protect others from fraud. Always stay cautious and trust your instincts when something feels off.
