Tax scams often involve fake messages claiming you owe money or are due a refund. These scams can come as emails, phone calls, or texts pretending to be from tax authorities. Common warning signs include urgent demands for payment, threats of arrest, requests for personal information, or payment via unusual methods like gift cards or wire transfers. If you receive a suspicious message, do not respond, click links, or provide personal details. Instead, contact your tax agency directly using official contact information to verify the claim. Reporting the scam to the proper authorities helps protect others from falling victim. Always stay cautious and double-check any unexpected tax-related communication.
