Scammers often pretend to be from Bank of America, sending fake emails, texts, or calls to steal personal information. These messages may claim there’s a problem with your account or urgent action is needed. Common warning signs include unexpected requests for passwords, Social Security numbers, or a sense of urgency to respond quickly. The sender’s contact details may look unusual or have spelling errors. If you receive such a message, do not click links or provide information. Instead, contact the bank directly using official phone numbers or their website to verify. Reporting suspicious activity helps protect you and others from fraud. Stay cautious and always confirm before sharing sensitive details.
