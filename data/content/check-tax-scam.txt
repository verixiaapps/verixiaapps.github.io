A common tax-related scam involves fake messages or calls claiming you’re owed a refund or prize. Scammers often ask for personal information or payment to "release" a check or refund. Warning signs include unsolicited contact, urgent demands, requests for payment via gift cards or wire transfer, and poor grammar or spelling. If you receive such a message, do not share personal details or send money. Instead, verify the claim by contacting the official tax agency directly using trusted contact information. Reporting the incident to the tax authority and local consumer protection agencies can also help protect others from falling victim. Stay cautious and always confirm before responding to unexpected requests.
