Scammers often send fake messages pretending to be a popular delivery company, saying you missed a package or need to pay a fee. These messages may include links or ask for personal information. Common warning signs include poor spelling, urgent requests for money, unfamiliar sender addresses, and unexpected notifications about deliveries you didn’t order. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or provide details. Instead, check your delivery status directly through the official company website or contact their customer service. Reporting suspicious messages helps protect others from falling victim to fraud. Always stay cautious with unexpected delivery notices.
