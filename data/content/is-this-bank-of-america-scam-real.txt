Scammers often send fake messages pretending to be from Bank of America, claiming there’s an issue with your account or asking for personal info. These scams usually include urgent language and links to fake websites. Common warning signs are unexpected requests for passwords or Social Security numbers, poor grammar, and email addresses that don’t match the official domain. If you receive such a message, don’t click any links or share personal details. Instead, contact Bank of America directly using a phone number or website you trust to verify the message. Always stay cautious to protect your information from fraud.
