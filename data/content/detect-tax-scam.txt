Tax scams often involve fake calls, emails, or letters claiming you owe money or must act quickly. Scammers may pretend to be government agents, using threats or urgent language to pressure you. Common warning signs include demands for immediate payment, requests for personal information, or payment via unusual methods like gift cards or wire transfers. Legitimate tax authorities do not threaten arrest or ask for payment through these channels. If you receive such a message, do not share personal details or send money. Instead, verify the claim by contacting your tax agency directly using official contact information. Reporting suspicious contacts helps protect others from falling victim. Staying cautious can prevent financial loss and identity theft.
