Remote job scams often promise easy work and quick money but require upfront fees or personal information. They may use official-sounding company names or vague job descriptions. Common warning signs include requests for payment, asking for sensitive data early, unrealistic salary offers, and poor grammar in messages. Scammers may pressure you to act fast or avoid interviews. If you receive a suspicious offer, verify the company through official websites, refuse to pay any fees, and never share personal details. Report the scam to job platforms and authorities to help protect others from fraud. Stay cautious and trust your instincts when applying for remote positions.
