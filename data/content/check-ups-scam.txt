A common scam involves fake medical check-up offers, often through calls, emails, or texts. Scammers claim you need urgent tests or exams and ask for personal information or payment upfront. Warning signs include unsolicited contact, pressure to act quickly, requests for sensitive data, and demands for payment by unusual methods like gift cards or wire transfers. If you receive such a message, don’t share any personal details or payment info. Instead, verify the source by contacting your doctor or healthcare provider directly using official contact details. Reporting suspicious activity to local authorities or consumer protection agencies can help protect others from falling victim. Always stay cautious and confirm before agreeing to any medical service offers.
