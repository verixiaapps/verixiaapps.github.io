Phishing emails often pretend to be from trusted sources like banks or popular companies. They usually ask you to click a link or provide personal information. Common warning signs include spelling mistakes, urgent or threatening language, unfamiliar sender addresses, and links that don’t match the official website. If you receive a suspicious message, don’t click any links or download attachments. Instead, verify the sender by contacting the company directly through their official website or phone number. Always keep your software updated and consider using security tools to help detect scams. Staying cautious helps protect your personal information and devices from fraud.
