Scams pretending to be from Bank of America often come as urgent emails, texts, or calls claiming there’s a problem with your account. They may ask for personal information, passwords, or direct you to fake websites. Common warning signs include poor spelling, suspicious links, requests for sensitive data, and unsolicited contact. Legitimate messages usually address you by name and do not ask for passwords or full Social Security numbers. If you receive such communication, do not click any links or share information. Instead, contact Bank of America directly using official phone numbers or their website to verify. Reporting the scam to the bank helps protect others from falling victim.
