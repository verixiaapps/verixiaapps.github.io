IRS-related scams often involve phone calls or emails claiming you owe money and must pay immediately. Scammers may threaten arrest, demand payment via gift cards or wire transfer, or ask for personal information. Common warning signs include unexpected requests for payment, limited time to pay, or pressure to provide sensitive details. Legitimate tax authorities usually communicate by mail and never demand immediate payment over the phone. If you receive a suspicious message, do not respond or share personal data. Instead, contact the official tax agency directly using verified contact information to confirm. Reporting the attempt to the appropriate authorities can help protect others from falling victim. Stay alert and verify before taking any action.
