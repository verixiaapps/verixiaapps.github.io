Scams pretending to be from Bank of America often involve fake emails, texts, or calls claiming there’s a problem with your account. They may ask for personal details or urge you to click suspicious links. Common warning signs include poor spelling, urgent messages, unknown phone numbers, and requests for passwords or social security numbers. Legitimate representatives never ask for sensitive information this way. If you receive a suspicious message, do not respond or click links. Instead, contact the bank directly using official phone numbers or their website to verify the message. Reporting the scam to the bank and local authorities helps protect others from falling victim. Always stay cautious and protect your personal information.
