Fake job offers often promise easy money or high-paying work with little effort. Scammers may ask for personal information, upfront fees, or payments for training or equipment. Common warning signs include offers that sound too good to be true, requests for money before you start, poor grammar in messages, and vague job details. If you receive a suspicious offer, do not share personal or financial information. Research the company independently and contact them directly to verify the job’s legitimacy. Report the suspicious message to job sites or authorities to help protect others. Always trust your instincts and avoid rushing into decisions about employment offers.
