Scam emails often pretend to be from trusted sources like banks or popular companies. They may ask for personal information, prompt urgent action, or offer too-good-to-be-true deals. Common warning signs include poor grammar, unusual sender addresses, unexpected attachments, and links that don’t match the supposed source. If you receive a suspicious message, avoid clicking links or downloading files. Instead, verify the sender’s identity by contacting the company directly through official channels. Report the email to your service provider or relevant authorities to help protect others. Staying cautious helps prevent fraud and keeps your information safe.
