Scams pretending to be from the postal service often involve fake delivery notices or urgent payment requests for packages. They may ask for personal information or prompt you to click suspicious links. Common warning signs include poor grammar, unfamiliar sender addresses, unexpected fees, and pressure to act quickly. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or provide personal details. Instead, verify the claim by contacting the official postal service directly through their website or customer service number. Reporting the suspicious message to authorities helps protect others from falling victim to these scams. Always stay cautious when dealing with unexpected communication about your mail.
