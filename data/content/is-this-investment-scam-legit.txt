Investment scams often promise high returns with little risk, using urgent language to push quick decisions. They may come through emails, calls, or social media, featuring fake endorsements or unrealistic profit claims. Common warning signs include pressure to act fast, requests for upfront payments, lack of clear contact information, and offers that seem too good to be true. If you receive such an offer, do not share personal or financial details. Instead, research the company thoroughly, check with official financial regulators, and seek advice from trusted financial professionals before taking any action. Staying cautious can protect you from losing money to fraudulent schemes.
