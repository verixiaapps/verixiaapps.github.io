IRS-related scams often involve fake calls, emails, or texts claiming you owe money or will face arrest. Scammers may send counterfeit refund checks or demand personal information. Common warning signs include threats of immediate arrest, requests for payment via gift cards or wire transfers, and emails with poor spelling or suspicious links. The IRS never demands payment this way or threatens legal action without prior official letters. If you receive such communication, do not provide personal details or send money. Instead, verify the claim by contacting the IRS directly using official phone numbers or their website. Reporting the incident to the Treasury Inspector General for Tax Administration (TIGTA) helps protect others from fraud.
