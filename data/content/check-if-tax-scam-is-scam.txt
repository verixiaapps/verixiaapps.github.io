Tax-related scams often involve calls, emails, or messages claiming you owe money or face legal trouble. Scammers may use threats, urgent language, or ask for personal info and payment via unusual methods like gift cards or wire transfers.

Common warning signs include unsolicited contact, pressure to pay immediately, requests for personal details, and errors in official logos or email addresses.

If you receive such a message, don’t respond or provide information. Instead, verify by contacting the tax authority directly using official contact details. Report suspicious activity to local authorities or tax fraud hotlines to help protect yourself and others.
