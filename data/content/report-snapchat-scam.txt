A Snapchat scam often involves messages from fake accounts pretending to be friends or popular users, asking for money, personal information, or login details. Common warning signs include urgent requests for cash, suspicious links, spelling errors, and unfamiliar profiles. If you receive such a message, do not respond or click any links. Instead, block the sender and report the account directly through Snapchat’s reporting tools. It’s also wise to change your password and enable two-factor authentication to protect your account. Staying alert helps keep you safe from these deceptive tactics.
