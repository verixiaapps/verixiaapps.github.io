A common PayPal scam involves fake emails or messages claiming there’s an issue with your account or a suspicious payment. These messages often ask you to click a link and enter personal information. Warning signs include poor grammar, urgent language, unfamiliar sender addresses, and requests for passwords or payment details. The messages may look official but often have subtle errors or suspicious links. If you receive such a message, avoid clicking any links or providing information. Instead, log into your PayPal account directly from the official website or app to check for alerts. You can also forward suspicious emails to spoof@paypal.com and delete them to stay safe.
