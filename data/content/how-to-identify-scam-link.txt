Scam links often appear as urgent messages or too-good-to-be-true offers from unknown sources. They may mimic trusted websites but have unusual URLs or spelling errors. Common warning signs include unexpected requests for personal information, suspicious attachments, or pressure to act quickly. If you receive such a link, avoid clicking on it. Instead, verify the sender’s identity through a trusted channel. Use security software to scan messages and report the link to your email provider or relevant authorities. Staying cautious helps protect your personal data and devices from harm.
