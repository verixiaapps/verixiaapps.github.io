Scammers using Zelle often pose as trusted contacts or businesses, asking for quick payments or refunds. They may claim urgent situations, like overpayments or prize winnings, pressuring you to send money immediately. Common warning signs include unfamiliar requests, pressure to act fast, or payments to unknown people. If you receive a suspicious message, do not send money or share personal information. Instead, verify the request by contacting the person or company directly using a trusted method. Report the incident to your bank and Zelle’s support team right away to help prevent fraud and protect your accounts. Always stay cautious with unexpected payment requests.
