Scam links often appear as urgent messages claiming you’ve won a prize, need to verify your account, or must act quickly to avoid problems. They may use poor spelling, strange URLs, or unexpected attachments. Common warning signs include unknown senders, requests for personal information, and offers that seem too good to be true. If you receive a suspicious message, avoid clicking any links or downloading files. Instead, verify the source by contacting the company directly through official channels. Reporting the message to your email provider or relevant authorities can help protect others. Stay cautious and double-check before interacting with unexpected online requests.
