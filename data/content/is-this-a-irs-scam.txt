Scammers often impersonate the IRS to trick people into paying fake taxes or fees. These calls or messages usually claim you owe money and threaten arrest or legal action if you don’t pay immediately. Common warning signs include demands for payment via gift cards, prepaid debit cards, or wire transfers, and pressure to act quickly without time to ask questions. The IRS rarely contacts taxpayers by phone first and will never ask for personal information this way. If you receive such a message, do not provide any details or send money. Instead, hang up and report it to the official IRS website or call their real customer service line for verification. Staying cautious can protect you from fraud.
