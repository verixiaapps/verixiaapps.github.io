Scam offers often promise high returns with little risk, using pressure tactics or unclear details. They may come through emails, phone calls, or social media, asking for upfront fees or personal information. Common warning signs include promises that sound too good to be true, unsolicited messages, requests for quick decisions, and lack of verifiable credentials. If you receive such an offer, avoid sharing personal data and do not send money. Research the company through official sources, check reviews, and consult trusted financial advisors. Reporting suspicious activity to consumer protection agencies can help protect others from falling victim. Staying cautious helps ensure your money stays safe.
