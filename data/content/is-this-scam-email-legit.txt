Scam emails often appear urgent, asking for personal info, passwords, or money. They may look official, using logos and formal language but contain spelling errors or strange email addresses. Common warning signs include unexpected requests, urgent threats, or offers that seem too good to be true. If you receive such a message, don’t click any links or download attachments. Instead, verify the sender by contacting the company directly through official channels. Report the email to your email provider or local authorities to help protect others. Staying cautious is key to avoiding fraud and keeping your information safe.
