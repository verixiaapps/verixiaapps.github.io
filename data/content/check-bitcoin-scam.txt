Bitcoin scams often promise quick profits or ask for upfront payments to unlock funds. They may come as emails, messages, or fake websites impersonating trusted sources. Common warning signs include requests for personal info, urgent pressure to act, promises of guaranteed returns, and demands for payment via untraceable methods. If you encounter such offers, do not share your details or send money. Instead, verify the source independently and report suspicious activity to relevant authorities or platforms. Staying cautious helps protect your assets from fraud.
