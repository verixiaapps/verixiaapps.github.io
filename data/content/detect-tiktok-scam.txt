Scams on TikTok often appear as messages or videos promising prizes, job offers, or quick money in exchange for personal information or payments. Common warning signs include unsolicited messages, requests for passwords or payment details, and links that look suspicious or lead outside the app. Scammers may use fake profiles or claim to be official representatives. If you come across something like this, avoid clicking any links or sharing personal data. Report the suspicious account or content directly to TikTok using the app’s reporting tools. Stay cautious and verify offers through official channels before taking action to protect your information and avoid fraud.
