Investment scams often promise high returns with little risk and use urgent language to pressure quick decisions. They may come as unsolicited calls, emails, or social media messages offering too-good-to-be-true deals. Common warning signs include unrealistic profits, lack of clear information, requests for personal or financial details, and unlicensed sellers. If you encounter such an offer, do not share any sensitive information or send money. Instead, research the company through official financial authorities and consult a trusted advisor. Reporting suspicious activity to consumer protection agencies helps prevent others from falling victim. Staying cautious and informed is key to protecting your finances.
