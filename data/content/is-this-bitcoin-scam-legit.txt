Scams involving Bitcoin often promise quick profits or guaranteed returns, asking for upfront payments or personal information. They may appear as messages from trusted sources or official-looking websites. Common warning signs include unrealistic offers, pressure to act fast, requests for your wallet keys, or unsolicited contact. If you receive such an offer, do not share any personal or financial details. Verify the source independently and avoid clicking links or downloading attachments. Report the suspicious message to relevant authorities or platforms. Staying cautious helps protect your money and personal data from fraud.
