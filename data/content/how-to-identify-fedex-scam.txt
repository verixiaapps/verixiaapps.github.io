Scammers often send fake messages pretending to be from FedEx, claiming there’s a problem with a package. These messages may ask for personal information, payment, or clicking suspicious links. Common warning signs include unexpected delivery alerts, poor spelling or grammar, urgent requests for money, and sender addresses that don’t match official FedEx contacts. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or provide details. Instead, visit the official FedEx website or contact their customer service directly to verify any delivery issues. Always be cautious with unexpected emails or texts about shipments, especially if they request payment or sensitive information.
