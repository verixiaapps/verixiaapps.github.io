Scam links often appear as urgent messages, fake offers, or unexpected requests asking you to click quickly. They may mimic trusted brands or use unusual web addresses. Common warning signs include poor spelling, suspicious sender addresses, and promises that seem too good to be true. If you receive a suspicious link, do not click on it. Instead, verify the source by contacting the company directly through official channels. Avoid sharing personal information and run a security scan on your device. When in doubt, delete the message and report it to your email provider or IT department to help protect others.
