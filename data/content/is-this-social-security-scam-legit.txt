Scammers often pretend to be from Social Security, sending calls or messages claiming problems with your account or benefits. They may ask for personal information, threaten legal action, or request payment. Warning signs include unsolicited contact, demands for immediate payment, requests for sensitive details like your Social Security number, or pressure to use unusual payment methods like gift cards. If you receive such a message, do not share any personal info or send money. Instead, hang up and contact the official Social Security Administration directly using their verified phone number or website to confirm. Staying cautious helps protect you from fraud and identity theft.
