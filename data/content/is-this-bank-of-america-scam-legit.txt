Scams pretending to be from Bank of America often involve fake emails, calls, or texts claiming there’s an issue with your account. They may ask for personal information or prompt you to click suspicious links. Common warning signs include urgent language, spelling errors, and requests for passwords or Social Security numbers. Legitimate banks never ask for sensitive details this way. If you receive such a message, don’t click any links or share information. Instead, contact the bank directly using official phone numbers or websites to verify the claim. Reporting the scam to the bank and relevant authorities helps protect others from falling victim. Stay cautious to keep your information safe.
