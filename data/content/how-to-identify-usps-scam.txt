USPS scams often come as unexpected emails, texts, or calls claiming there’s a problem with a package or delivery. These messages may ask for personal information, payment, or direct you to suspicious links. Common warning signs include poor grammar, urgent language, unfamiliar sender addresses, and requests for money or sensitive data. Official communications rarely demand immediate payment or sensitive details via email or text. If you receive such a message, do not click on any links or provide information. Instead, contact the postal service directly using official contact details from their website to verify the claim. Reporting the suspicious message helps protect others from fraud.
