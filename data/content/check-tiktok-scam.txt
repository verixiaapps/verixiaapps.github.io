Scams on TikTok often appear as fake giveaways, fraudulent shop links, or requests for personal information. They may promise prizes, ask for money upfront, or direct you to suspicious websites. Common warning signs include unbelievable offers, urgent messages pressuring quick action, poor spelling or grammar, and links from unknown accounts. If you encounter such messages, avoid clicking links or sharing details. Instead, report the content to TikTok through the app's reporting tools and block the sender. Stay cautious and verify offers through official channels to protect yourself from fraud.
