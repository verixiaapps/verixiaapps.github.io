Some scams pretending to be from Amazon involve fake emails or messages about suspicious orders or account issues. These usually ask you to click a link or provide personal information. Common signs include poor spelling, urgent language, mismatched email addresses, or unusual Sender names. Genuine messages from Amazon will never ask for sensitive details like passwords or payment information via email. If you get such a message, do not click any links or share information. Instead, log in directly to your official Amazon account through their website or app to check for alerts. You can also report the suspicious message to Amazon’s official help center for verification and assistance. Staying cautious helps protect your personal data.
