Remote job scams often promise easy work and high pay but ask for upfront fees or personal information. They may use fake company names or email addresses that look real. Common warning signs include requests for money, vague job descriptions, unrealistic salary offers, and pressure to act quickly. If you receive such an offer, verify the company’s official website, search for reviews, and never pay fees to get a job. Report suspicious messages to job platforms or authorities. Always protect your personal data and trust your instincts if something feels off.
