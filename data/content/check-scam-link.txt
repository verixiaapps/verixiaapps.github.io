Scam links often appear as urgent messages or offers, like fake prize wins, suspicious job offers, or unexpected requests for personal information. Common warning signs include poor grammar, strange web addresses, requests for passwords or money, and unfamiliar senders. These links may lead to fake websites designed to steal your data or install harmful software. If you receive a suspicious link, avoid clicking it. Instead, verify the sender’s identity separately, use official websites to check offers, and run security software on your device. Reporting the message to your email provider or platform helps protect others. Staying cautious can prevent falling victim to online fraud.
