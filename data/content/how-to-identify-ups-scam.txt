Scammers often pretend to be delivery companies, like UPS, claiming there's a problem with a package. They might ask for personal information, payment details, or prompt you to click suspicious links. Common warning signs include unexpected messages, poor grammar, urgent demands for payment, and requests for sensitive data. Legitimate companies never ask for payment via unusual methods like gift cards or wire transfers. If you receive such a message, do not respond or click any links. Instead, verify the shipment by contacting the delivery company directly using information from their official website. Reporting the message to UPS and local authorities can help protect others from falling victim to these scams.
