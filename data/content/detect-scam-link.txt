Scam links often appear as urgent messages or offers that seem too good to be true, such as prize notifications or fake security alerts. Common warning signs include suspicious URLs with strange spellings, unexpected messages from unknown senders, poor grammar, and requests for personal information. Links that ask you to download files or enter passwords should raise caution. If you receive a suspicious message, avoid clicking any links or downloading attachments. Instead, verify the source through official channels or contact the company directly. Reporting the message to your email provider or relevant authorities can help protect others from falling victim. Staying alert and cautious online helps keep your information safe.
