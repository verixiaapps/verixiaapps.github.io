Scam emails often pretend to be from trusted sources like banks or popular companies. They usually ask for personal info or urgent action, such as clicking a link or providing passwords. Common warning signs include poor grammar, unfamiliar sender addresses, unexpected attachments, and pressure to act quickly. If you receive a suspicious message, avoid clicking links or downloading files. Instead, verify by contacting the company directly using official contact details. Delete the email if it seems fake, and consider reporting it to your email provider or relevant authorities to help protect others. Staying cautious can keep your information safe.
