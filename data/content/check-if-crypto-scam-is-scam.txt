Crypto scams often promise quick, huge profits or exclusive investment opportunities. They may ask for upfront payments, private keys, or personal information. Common warning signs include unrealistic returns, urgent requests, and unsolicited messages from unknown sources. Be cautious of poorly designed websites or offers that sound too good to be true. If you receive such a message or offer, do not share personal details or send money. Instead, verify the sender’s identity through official channels, report the suspicious activity to relevant authorities, and seek advice from trusted sources. Staying informed and skeptical can protect you from losing funds to fraudulent schemes.
