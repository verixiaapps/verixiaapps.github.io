IRS scams often involve fake calls, emails, or messages claiming you owe taxes and must pay immediately to avoid arrest. Scammers may threaten you or ask for personal information. Common warning signs include urgent demands for payment, requests for gift cards or wire transfers, and caller ID spoofing to appear official. The IRS will never demand immediate payment without notice or ask for payment via unusual methods. If you receive such a message, do not respond or provide information. Instead, report it to the IRS directly at their official website or call their official phone number. Always verify any tax-related contact independently to protect yourself from fraud.
