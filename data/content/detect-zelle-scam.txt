Zelle scams often involve criminals pretending to be someone you trust or offering fake deals to trick you into sending money quickly. Common warning signs include urgent requests for payment, unfamiliar contacts asking for money, or unexpected messages claiming you owe money. Scammers may also pressure you not to verify details or mention using third-party apps. If you receive such messages, do not respond or send money. Instead, verify the request by contacting the person directly through a trusted method. Report suspicious activity to your bank and Zelle immediately to protect your account. Staying cautious and double-checking all payment requests can help you avoid falling victim to these scams.
