Scammers often pretend to be from Amazon, sending fake emails or messages about orders, refunds, or account issues. These messages usually ask for personal information, payment details, or prompt you to click suspicious links. Common warning signs include poor grammar, urgent tone, unfamiliar email addresses, and requests for sensitive data. Genuine Amazon communications typically don’t ask for passwords or payment info through messages. If you receive such contact, do not click any links or provide information. Instead, verify the message by logging directly into your official Amazon account or contacting Amazon customer service. Report the suspicious message to Amazon to help protect others. Staying cautious helps keep your account and personal data safe.
