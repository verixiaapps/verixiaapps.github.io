Crypto scams often promise big returns or free coins to lure people in. They might use fake websites, phishing emails, or social media messages pretending to be trustworthy sources. Common warning signs include unsolicited offers, requests for private keys or passwords, unrealistic profit claims, and pressure to act quickly. If you receive such a message, do not share any personal information or send money. Instead, verify the sender’s identity through official channels and report the suspicious activity to relevant authorities or platforms. Staying cautious and informed helps protect your assets from fraud.
