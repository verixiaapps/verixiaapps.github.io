Scam messages often mimic emails from trusted sources, like banks or popular companies, to trick you into giving personal information or money. They may contain urgent language, spelling mistakes, or suspicious links asking you to verify accounts or payments. Common warning signs include unfamiliar sender addresses, requests for sensitive details, and unexpected attachments. If you receive such a message, avoid clicking links or downloading files. Do not reply or provide any personal data. Instead, verify the request by contacting the company directly using official contact information. Reporting the message to your email provider or relevant authorities can help protect others from falling victim. Stay cautious and double-check before taking any action.
