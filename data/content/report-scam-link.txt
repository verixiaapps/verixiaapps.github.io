Scam links often appear as urgent messages or offers from unknown sources, asking you to click a link. They may look like official emails, texts, or social media messages but usually contain spelling errors or strange URLs. Common warning signs include unexpected requests for personal information, promises that seem too good to be true, and links that don’t match the company they claim to represent. If you receive a suspicious message, avoid clicking the link. Instead, delete the message and report it to your email provider or the relevant platform. Staying cautious helps protect your personal data and prevents fraud.
