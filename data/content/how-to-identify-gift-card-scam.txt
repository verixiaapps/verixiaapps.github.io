Gift card scams often involve someone asking you to pay with gift cards for urgent or unusual reasons, like fake debts or prizes. Common warning signs include pressure to act quickly, requests for specific gift card brands, and instructions to share the card numbers or photos. Scammers may contact you by phone, email, or social media, claiming to be officials or company representatives. If you receive this kind of request, don’t buy or share gift cards. Instead, verify the person’s identity independently and report the incident to local authorities or consumer protection agencies. Staying alert can help protect your money and personal information.
