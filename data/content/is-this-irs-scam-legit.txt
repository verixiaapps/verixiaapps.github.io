Scammers often impersonate the IRS to trick people into paying fake debts or providing personal information. These calls or emails usually pressure you to act quickly, threaten arrest or legal action, and demand payment via gift cards, wire transfers, or prepaid debit cards. Warning signs include requests for immediate payment, demands for unusual payment methods, and callers refusing to provide official contact information. The real IRS will not call unexpectedly or threaten arrest. If you receive such a message, do not share personal details or send money. Instead, contact the IRS directly using official phone numbers or visit their website to verify any claims. Reporting the incident to the FTC can also help protect others.
