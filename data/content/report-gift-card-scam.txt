Gift card scams often involve someone asking for payment using prepaid cards, claiming it’s urgent or secret. Scammers may pretend to be officials, tech support, or family in trouble. Common warning signs include pressure to pay quickly, requests for gift card numbers or codes, and unusual or threatening messages. If you receive such a request, don’t share any card information. Instead, verify the sender’s identity through trusted sources and report the incident to local authorities or consumer protection agencies. Remember, legitimate organizations rarely ask for payments this way. Staying cautious helps protect your money and personal information.
