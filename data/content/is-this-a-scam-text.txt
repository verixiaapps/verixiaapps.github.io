Scam messages often try to trick you into giving personal information or money. They may claim you won a prize, warn about a fake problem with your account, or offer too-good-to-be-true deals. Common warning signs include urgent language, requests for passwords or payment details, strange sender numbers, and poor spelling or grammar. If you receive a suspicious message, don’t reply or click any links. Instead, delete it and report it to your phone carrier or a trusted organization. Always verify offers or alerts by contacting companies directly using official contact info. Staying cautious helps protect your personal data and money from fraud.
