Many scams claim to be from Wells Fargo, often by phone, email, or text. Scammers say there’s a problem with your account or ask for personal info to "verify" identity. Common warning signs include urgent requests, spelling mistakes, or unfamiliar links. Legitimate Wells Fargo messages usually address you by name and never ask for sensitive data via email or text. If you receive a suspicious message, do not click any links or share details. Instead, contact Wells Fargo directly using the official website or phone number to confirm. Reporting the scam to the bank and relevant authorities helps protect others from fraud. Stay cautious and always verify before responding.
