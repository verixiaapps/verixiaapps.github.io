Remote job scams often promise easy work and high pay but ask for personal information or money upfront. Typical signs include offers that seem too good to be true, requests for payment to start, poor grammar in messages, and vague company details. Scammers may contact you quickly and pressure you to act fast. If you receive an offer, research the company independently, verify job listings on official sites, and never share sensitive data or send money before confirming legitimacy. Reporting suspicious contacts to job platforms or local authorities helps protect others. Stay cautious and trust your instincts when something feels off.
