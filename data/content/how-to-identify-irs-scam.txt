IRS-related scams often involve phone calls, emails, or texts claiming you owe money or face legal action. Scammers may threaten arrest, demand immediate payment, or ask for personal information. Common warning signs include requests for payment via gift cards or wire transfers, caller ID spoofing official-looking numbers, and aggressive or threatening language. The government typically contacts taxpayers by mail first, not phone or email. If you receive such a message, do not provide personal information or make payments. Instead, hang up and verify by contacting the official tax agency directly using trusted phone numbers or websites. Reporting the incident to authorities helps protect others from falling victim.
