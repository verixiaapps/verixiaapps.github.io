Wells Fargo scams often appear as fake calls, texts, or emails claiming there’s a problem with your account. Scammers may ask for personal information, passwords, or payment details. Common warning signs include urgent requests, suspicious links, spelling errors, and messages from unofficial addresses. Legitimate Wells Fargo communications will never ask for sensitive information via email or text. If you receive a suspicious message, do not click on any links or provide details. Instead, contact Wells Fargo directly using the official phone number on their website. You can also report the scam to the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) to help protect others. Staying cautious helps keep your information safe.
