Zelle scams often involve messages or calls claiming you owe money or are due a refund, urging quick payment or personal details. Scammers may impersonate banks, friends, or government agencies. Common warning signs include pressure to pay immediately, requests for personal information, unusual payment requests, and errors in grammar or spelling. If you suspect a scam, do not reply or send money. Instead, verify the request by contacting the person or organization directly using official contact information. Report suspicious activity to your bank and the platform where the transaction occurred. Staying cautious helps protect your money and personal information.
