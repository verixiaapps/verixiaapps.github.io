Scammers pretending to be from Wells Fargo often contact people by phone, email, or text, claiming there’s a problem with their account or asking for personal information. These messages may sound urgent or threatening to get you to act quickly. Common warning signs include unexpected requests for passwords, Social Security numbers, or bank details, spelling mistakes, and links that don’t look official. If you receive such a message, do not reply or click any links. Instead, contact Wells Fargo directly using a verified phone number or their official website to check your account. Reporting the incident helps protect you and others from fraud.
