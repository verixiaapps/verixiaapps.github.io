A common scam involves calls or messages pretending to be from Social Security, often claiming your account is suspended or there is suspicious activity. Scammers usually ask for personal information or payment. Warning signs include threats, urgent demands, requests for your Social Security number, or payment via gift cards or wire transfer. Legitimate agencies do not ask for sensitive details over the phone or threaten immediate action. If you receive such a call or message, do not provide any information. Instead, hang up and contact the official Social Security office directly using their verified phone number or website to report the incident and verify your account status. Staying cautious helps protect your identity and finances.
