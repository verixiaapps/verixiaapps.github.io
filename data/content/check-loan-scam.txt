A common loan scam involves fake offers promising easy money, often requiring an upfront fee or personal information. Scammers may send a check that looks real but is fake or will bounce after you deposit it. Warning signs include requests for payment before approval, unsolicited offers, pressure to act quickly, and poor grammar or suspicious email addresses. If you receive such an offer, do not share personal details or send money. Verify the lender through official channels and report suspicious activity to consumer protection agencies. Always be cautious and trust trusted financial institutions to avoid falling victim to fraud.
