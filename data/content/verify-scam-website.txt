Scam sites often mimic real websites to steal personal information or money. They may offer deals that seem too good to be true or ask for sensitive details like passwords and credit card numbers. Watch out for warning signs such as poor spelling and grammar, suspicious URLs, lack of contact information, and requests for unusual payments like gift cards. If you suspect a site is fraudulent, avoid clicking links, do not enter any personal data, and run a web search to check reviews or warnings. Report the site to your browser or consumer protection agency to help protect others from falling victim. Always stay cautious and double-check before trusting unfamiliar online offers.
