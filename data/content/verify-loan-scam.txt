A loan scam often involves fake offers promising easy money with low interest or no credit checks. Scammers may ask for upfront fees, personal details, or bank information. Common warning signs include unsolicited messages, pressure to pay fees quickly, requests for sensitive data, and unclear or untraceable company details. If you receive such an offer, do not share personal information or send money. Instead, verify the lender's authenticity by checking official websites or contacting financial authorities. Report suspicious activity to consumer protection agencies to help prevent others from falling victim. Always be cautious with loan offers that seem too good to be true.
