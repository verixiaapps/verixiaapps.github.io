Scams pretending to be from Bank of America often involve fake calls, emails, or texts claiming there is a problem with your account. They may ask for personal information or urge you to click suspicious links. Common warning signs include urgent messages, requests for passwords or Social Security numbers, and unfamiliar phone numbers or email addresses. Legitimate bank communications usually don’t ask for sensitive details this way. If you get a suspicious message, don’t respond or click any links. Instead, contact Bank of America directly using the phone number on the official website or your bank statement to confirm if the message is real. Reporting the scam helps protect others from fraud.
