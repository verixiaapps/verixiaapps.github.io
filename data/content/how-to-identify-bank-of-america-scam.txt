Bank of America scams often come as unexpected calls, emails, or texts asking for personal info or claiming urgent issues with your account. They may use official logos and threaten account closure or suspicious charges to create panic. Common warning signs include poor grammar, requests for passwords or Social Security numbers, and links directing you to unofficial websites. Legitimate communications rarely ask for sensitive details via email or phone. If you receive such a message, don’t click any links or share information. Instead, contact Bank of America directly using a trusted phone number or official website to verify the message’s authenticity. Reporting the scam helps protect others from fraud.
