Scammers pretending to be from Wells Fargo often send emails, texts, or calls claiming there’s a problem with your account. They might ask for personal info, passwords, or payment details. Common warning signs include urgent messages, spelling errors, suspicious links, and requests for sensitive data. Legitimate Wells Fargo communications never ask for your full password or PIN. If you receive a suspicious message, do not click on links or provide info. Instead, contact Wells Fargo directly using official phone numbers or their website to verify the message. Reporting the scam to the bank helps protect others from falling victim. Always stay cautious and protect your personal information.
