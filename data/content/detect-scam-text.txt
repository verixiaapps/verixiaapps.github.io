Scam messages often try to trick you into giving personal information or money. They may claim you won a prize, face a problem with your account, or need to act quickly. Common warning signs include poor spelling and grammar, unknown senders, urgent language, and suspicious links or attachments. If you get one, do not respond or click any links. Instead, delete the message and report it to your phone carrier or relevant authorities. Always verify offers or requests by contacting companies directly through official channels. Staying cautious helps protect your personal information and prevents fraud.
