Scams claiming to be from Amazon often involve fake emails or messages stating there is an issue with your account or order, urging you to click a link or provide personal info. Common warning signs include poor spelling, urgent language, unfamiliar sender addresses, and requests for passwords or payment details. Real notifications from Amazon usually address you by name and never ask for sensitive information via email. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or share details. Instead, go directly to the official Amazon website or app to check your account. Reporting suspicious messages to Amazon helps protect others from fraud. Stay cautious to keep your personal information safe.
