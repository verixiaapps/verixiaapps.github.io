These scams often promise huge returns or ask for a small payment in Ethereum to unlock bigger rewards. They may appear as messages from fake celebrities or official accounts. Common warning signs include requests for personal keys, urgent pressure to send crypto, or offers that sound too good to be true. If you receive such an offer, do not share your wallet information or send any funds. Instead, verify the source through official channels and report suspicious messages to the platform or authorities. Staying cautious and informed helps protect your digital assets from fraud.
