Scams pretending to be from Chase often come as urgent emails, texts, or calls asking for personal information or bank details. They may claim there’s a problem with your account or a suspicious transaction. Common warning signs include poor grammar, requests for passwords or Social Security numbers, unfamiliar links, and a sense of urgency to act quickly. Official communications rarely ask for sensitive information this way. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or provide details. Instead, contact Chase directly using a phone number or website you trust to verify the message’s legitimacy. Reporting the scam to Chase and relevant authorities helps protect yourself and others from fraud.
