Fake job offers often promise high pay for little work or ask for personal information upfront. Scammers may use official-sounding emails and company logos to seem real. Common warning signs include requests for money, vague job details, poor grammar, and unsolicited offers. If you receive a suspicious message, do not share personal data or send money. Instead, verify the company’s official website or contact them directly using trusted contact information. Reporting the scam to job platforms or consumer protection agencies helps protect others. Always trust your instincts and research thoroughly before responding to any unexpected job opportunities.
