Phishing emails often pretend to be from trustworthy sources like banks or popular companies, asking you to click links or provide personal information. These messages usually create a sense of urgency, such as warning about an account issue or a prize. Common signs include poor spelling, suspicious sender addresses, unexpected attachments, and generic greetings like "Dear Customer." If you receive a suspicious message, avoid clicking any links or downloading files. Instead, verify the request by contacting the company directly using official contact details. Reporting the email to your IT department or email provider can help protect others from falling victim to the scam. Staying cautious helps keep your information safe.
