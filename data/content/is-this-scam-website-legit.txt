Scam websites often imitate real businesses to trick visitors into sharing personal or financial information. They usually have poor design, misspellings, and unusual web addresses. Common warning signs include offers that seem too good to be true, requests for upfront payments, and lack of clear contact details. If you come across a suspicious site, avoid clicking on any links or downloading files. Do not provide any personal information. Instead, research the company through trusted sources and look for reviews from other users. Reporting the site to your browser or a consumer protection agency can help protect others from falling victim. Always stay cautious and verify before trusting unfamiliar websites.
