Scams involving Social Security often come as phone calls or emails claiming there is a problem with your account or benefits. Scammers may say your number was used in a crime or that you owe money. Common warning signs include urgent threats, requests for personal information, or payments via gift cards or wire transfers. Official agencies will never ask for sensitive details this way or demand immediate payment. If you get a suspicious message, do not respond, share personal info, or click links. Instead, contact the Social Security Administration directly using official phone numbers or websites to verify the claim. Reporting the incident to authorities can help protect others from fraud.
