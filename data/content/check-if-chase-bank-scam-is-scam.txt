Scams pretending to be from Chase Bank often involve fake calls, emails, or texts claiming there’s an urgent problem with your account. They may ask for personal details, passwords, or payment information. Common warning signs include messages with spelling errors, requests for sensitive information, urgent threats, or unfamiliar links. Legitimate Chase communications never ask for your full password or PIN. If you receive a suspicious message, do not click any links or share information. Instead, contact Chase directly using the official phone number or website to verify. Always stay cautious to protect your personal and financial information from fraud.
