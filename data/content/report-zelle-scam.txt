Zelle scams often involve fake emails, texts, or calls asking for money or personal information. Scammers may pretend to be friends, family, or trusted companies, requesting quick payments. Common warning signs include urgent requests, unfamiliar contacts, spelling errors, or offers that seem too good to be true. If you receive a suspicious message asking for money, do not respond or provide any personal details. Instead, contact the person or company directly using known contact information. Report the incident to your bank and Zelle's fraud team to help prevent others from being targeted. Always verify requests before sending money to stay safe.
