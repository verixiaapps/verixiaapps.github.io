Scammers often impersonate postal services, claiming you have a package or unpaid fees. These messages may come as emails, texts, or phone calls asking for personal info or payment. Common warning signs include poor grammar, urgent threats, unfamiliar sender addresses, and requests for money or sensitive data. Official communications rarely demand immediate payment or ask for details via email or text. If you receive such a message, do not click links or provide information. Instead, visit the official postal service website or call their verified customer service number to confirm. Reporting suspicious contacts helps protect others from fraud. Stay cautious and verify before responding to unexpected delivery claims.
