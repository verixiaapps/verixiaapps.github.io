Scam messages often pretend to be from trusted companies, banks, or government agencies. They may ask for personal information, urgent payments, or contain suspicious links. Common warning signs include poor spelling, urgent language, unknown sender addresses, and requests for sensitive data. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or reply. Instead, verify the information by contacting the company directly using official contact details. Reporting the message to your email provider or relevant authorities can help protect others. Always stay cautious and double-check before trusting unexpected communications.
