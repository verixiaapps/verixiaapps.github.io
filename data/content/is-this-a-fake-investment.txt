A fake investment often promises high returns with little or no risk, usually through unsolicited calls, emails, or social media. These scams may ask for upfront fees or personal information. Common warning signs include pressure to act quickly, unrealistic profits, lack of clear details, and unlicensed sellers. If you receive such an offer, don’t respond or share personal information. Instead, research the company thoroughly, check with financial authorities, and seek advice from a trusted expert before making any decisions. Reporting suspicious activity helps protect others from falling victim to fraud.
