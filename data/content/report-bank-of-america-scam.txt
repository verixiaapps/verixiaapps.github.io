Scams involving Bank of America often come as fake calls, emails, or texts claiming there’s a problem with your account. They may ask for personal details, passwords, or money. Common warning signs include messages with urgent language, spelling mistakes, or suspicious links. The sender’s contact info might look unusual or unfamiliar. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or share your information. Instead, contact Bank of America directly using official phone numbers or their website to verify the claim. Reporting the scam helps protect others and allows the bank to take action against fraud. Always stay cautious to keep your information safe.
