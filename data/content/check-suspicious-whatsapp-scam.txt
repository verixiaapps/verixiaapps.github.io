Scammers on messaging apps often pose as friends, family, or official companies to trick users. These fake messages may ask for personal information, money, or urge you to click on suspicious links. Common warning signs include unexpected requests for money, spelling mistakes, unfamiliar numbers, or pressure to act quickly. If you receive a doubtful message, do not reply or click any links. Instead, verify by contacting the sender through a known, trusted method. Report the message to the app and block the sender to protect your information. Staying alert and cautious helps keep your account and personal data safe.
