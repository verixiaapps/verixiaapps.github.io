Scam links often appear as urgent messages claiming you’ve won a prize, need to update account details, or must verify personal information. They may look real but usually come from unknown sources or contain strange URLs. Common warning signs include poor spelling, unexpected requests for money or passwords, and links that don’t match the official website address. If you receive a suspicious message, avoid clicking the link. Instead, verify the information by contacting the company directly using known contact details. You can also use online tools to check the link’s safety. Reporting the scam to authorities helps protect others from falling victim. Always stay cautious and think twice before interacting with unexpected messages.
