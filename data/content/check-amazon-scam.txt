Amazon-related scams often involve fake emails, messages, or websites pretending to be from Amazon. They may ask for personal information, payment details, or prompt you to click suspicious links. Common warning signs include poor grammar, urgent requests, unfamiliar sender addresses, and offers that seem too good to be true. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or provide information. Instead, visit Amazon’s official site directly and check your account for alerts. Report the suspicious message to Amazon through their official channels to help protect yourself and others. Always verify communications carefully to avoid falling victim to fraud.
