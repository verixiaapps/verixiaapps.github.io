Bitcoin scams often involve fake offers promising high returns or requests for advance payments to unlock funds. These scams may come as emails, messages, or social media posts claiming urgent action is needed. Common warning signs include unsolicited contact, unrealistic profit guarantees, pressure to act quickly, and requests for personal information or payments upfront. Scammers may impersonate trusted figures or companies to appear legitimate. If you receive such an offer, do not respond or send money. Instead, verify the source independently and report the message to relevant authorities or platform administrators. Staying cautious and informed helps protect your finances from fraud.
