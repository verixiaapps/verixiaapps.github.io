Scam emails often appear urgent, asking for personal information or money. They may claim to be from banks, government agencies, or popular companies. Common warning signs include poor spelling and grammar, unfamiliar sender addresses, suspicious links, and unexpected attachments. These messages often create a sense of panic to rush you into action. If you receive such an email, do not click on any links or download attachments. Instead, verify the sender by contacting the company directly using official contact details. Reporting the message to your email provider or relevant authorities can help protect others. Always stay cautious and double-check before responding to unexpected requests online.
