Ethereum scams often promise big profits through fake investments, giveaways, or impersonating trusted sources. Common signs include urgent messages asking for private keys, requests to send cryptocurrency first, or too-good-to-be-true offers. Scammers may use official logos or usernames to seem legitimate. If you encounter this, do not respond or send any funds. Instead, report the incident to relevant platforms or authorities, such as the website where it happened or your local cybercrime unit. Always verify information through official channels and never share your private keys or passwords. Staying cautious helps protect your digital assets from fraud.
