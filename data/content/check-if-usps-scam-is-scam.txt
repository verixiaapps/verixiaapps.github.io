Scammers often impersonate the USPS to trick people into providing personal information or paying fake fees. These messages may claim there’s a package problem or a missed delivery, urging immediate action. Common warning signs include poor grammar, suspicious links, requests for money or sensitive data, and emails from unofficial addresses. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or provide information. Instead, visit the official USPS website or contact their customer service directly to verify any claims. Remember, the postal service typically does not ask for payment or personal details by email or text. Staying cautious helps protect you from fraud.
