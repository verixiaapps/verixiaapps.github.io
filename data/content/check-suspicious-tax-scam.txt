Tax scams often involve fake calls, emails, or messages claiming you owe money or face legal action. Scammers may use threats, urgent language, or ask for personal information and payment via unusual methods like gift cards or wire transfers. Common warning signs include unsolicited contact, requests for secrecy, and errors in official details. If you get such a message, don’t share personal data or pay money. Instead, verify the claim by contacting the official tax agency directly using trusted contact information. Reporting suspicious activity helps protect you and others from fraud. Always stay cautious and double-check before responding to unexpected tax-related requests.
