A common scam involves someone asking you to pay with Zelle for goods or services, then disputing the charge or disappearing without delivering. Often, the scammer pressures for quick payment or offers deals that seem too good to be true. Warning signs include unsolicited requests, urgent payment demands, and sellers who refuse to meet in person. If you receive such a request, verify the person's identity and avoid sending money to strangers. If you suspect fraud, contact your bank immediately to report it and consider notifying local authorities. Always use secure, traceable payment methods and stay cautious when dealing with unknown contacts.
