Scam investments often promise high returns with little or no risk, pushing people to act quickly. They might use fake websites, official-sounding documents, or pressure tactics to seem trustworthy. Common warning signs include guaranteed profits, unsolicited offers, lack of clear information, and requests for personal or payment details upfront. If you receive such an offer, do not share your information or send money. Instead, research the company through official channels, check for reviews or complaints, and consult a trusted financial advisor. Reporting suspicious activity to authorities can help protect others from falling victim. Always be cautious and take time to verify before making investment decisions.
