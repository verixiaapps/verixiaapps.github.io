Scams related to online shopping often appear as fake emails, messages, or phone calls claiming to be from a major retailer. They may ask for personal information, payment details, or prompt you to click suspicious links. Common warning signs include poor spelling or grammar, urgent requests for action, prices that seem too good to be true, and unfamiliar sender addresses. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or provide personal details. Instead, verify the information by contacting the company directly through their official website or customer service. Reporting the suspicious contact to the retailer can also help protect others from falling victim. Stay cautious and double-check before responding.
