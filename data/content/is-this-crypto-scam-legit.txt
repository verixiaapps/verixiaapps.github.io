Crypto scams often promise quick, high returns or free coins in exchange for an initial payment or personal information. They may appear as urgent messages, fake giveaways, or investment offers. Common warning signs include unrealistic profits, requests for private keys or passwords, and unverified or suspicious links. If you receive such an offer, do not share personal details or send money. Instead, verify the source through official channels, avoid clicking unknown links, and report the message to relevant authorities or platform support. Staying cautious protects your assets from fraud and loss.
