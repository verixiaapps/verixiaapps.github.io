Scam messages often appear as urgent alerts or offers that seem too good to be true. They may claim you’ve won a prize, owe money, or need to update personal details immediately. Common warning signs include poor spelling and grammar, suspicious links, requests for sensitive information, and unknown senders. If you receive a suspicious message, do not click on any links or provide personal details. Instead, verify the information by contacting the company or person directly using official channels. Report the message to your service provider or local authorities to help prevent others from falling victim. Staying cautious helps protect your personal data and finances from fraud.
