Tax-related scams often involve fake calls, emails, or messages claiming you owe money or will face arrest. Scammers may use aggressive language, demand immediate payment, or ask for personal details like Social Security numbers. Common warning signs include unexpected contact, threats of legal action, requests for payment via gift cards or wire transfer, and poor spelling or grammar. If you receive such a message, do not respond or provide any information. Instead, verify the claim by contacting the official tax agency directly using trusted phone numbers or websites. Reporting the suspicious communication to the authorities helps protect others from falling victim. Always stay cautious to avoid being tricked.
