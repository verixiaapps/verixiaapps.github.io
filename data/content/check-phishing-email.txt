Phishing scams often appear as urgent messages from trusted sources like banks or popular websites, asking you to click a link or provide personal information. Common warning signs include poor spelling, suspicious sender addresses, unexpected attachments, and requests for sensitive data. The message may also create a sense of urgency or threaten negative consequences. If you receive such an email, avoid clicking on any links or downloading attachments. Instead, verify the message by contacting the company directly using official contact details. Report the suspicious email to your email provider or IT department to help protect others. Staying cautious can prevent identity theft and keep your information safe.
