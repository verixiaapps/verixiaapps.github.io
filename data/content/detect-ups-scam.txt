A common scam involves fake messages claiming to be from UPS, saying there’s a problem with a delivery. These messages often ask you to click a link or provide personal information. Warning signs include poor spelling or grammar, urgent language pressuring you to act quickly, unfamiliar sender addresses, and unexpected requests for payment or details. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or share personal information. Instead, verify the delivery status directly on the official UPS website or contact their customer service. Reporting the suspicious message to UPS can help protect others from falling victim to similar fraud. Stay cautious to keep your information safe.
