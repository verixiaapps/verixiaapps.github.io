Many scams on WhatsApp involve messages claiming you've won a prize, need to verify your account, or offering too-good-to-be-true deals. These often ask for personal information, payment, or clicking suspicious links. Common warning signs include urgent language, requests for money or codes, unknown senders, and messages with spelling mistakes. If you receive such a message, avoid clicking links or sharing any details. Do not respond to the sender. Instead, verify the information independently through official sources. Reporting the message to WhatsApp and blocking the contact helps protect you and others from potential harm. Staying cautious helps keep your information and device safe.
