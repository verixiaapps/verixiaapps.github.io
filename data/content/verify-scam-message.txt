Scam messages often pretend to be urgent and come from banks, government agencies, or popular companies. They may ask for personal information, passwords, or payment details. Common warning signs include poor spelling, suspicious links, urgent language, and unexpected requests for money or personal data. If you receive a suspicious message, avoid clicking links or sharing information. Instead, verify its authenticity by contacting the company directly using official contact details. Report the message to your service provider or local authorities to help protect others. Staying cautious helps prevent falling victim to fraud.
