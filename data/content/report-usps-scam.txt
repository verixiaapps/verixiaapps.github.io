Scammers often pretend to be from the postal service, claiming you owe money for a package or need to verify personal information. These messages may come as emails, texts, or phone calls. Common signs include urgent language, unexpected fees, requests for personal details, and links or attachments asking for login info or payment. If you receive a suspicious message, do not click any links or provide information. Instead, verify the claim by contacting the official postal service directly through their website or customer service number. You can also report the incident to postal authorities or the Federal Trade Commission to help stop fraud and protect others.
