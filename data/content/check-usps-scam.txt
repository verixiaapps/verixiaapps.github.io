Scammers often send fake messages claiming there is an issue with a package delivery or payment. These messages may include urgent calls to click links, enter personal information, or pay fees. Common warning signs include poor grammar, unfamiliar sender addresses, unexpected requests for payment, and suspicious links. Official communications from the postal service rarely ask for sensitive details or payment through email or text. If you receive a suspicious message, do not click any links or share personal information. Instead, verify the package status directly on the official postal website or contact customer service. Reporting the scam to postal authorities can help protect others from falling victim. Stay cautious to avoid becoming a target.
