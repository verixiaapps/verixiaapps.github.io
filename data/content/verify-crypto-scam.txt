Crypto scams often promise quick, high returns or exclusive investment opportunities. They may appear as unsolicited messages, fake websites, or social media offers. Common warning signs include requests for personal information, pressure to act fast, unrealistic promises, and demands for upfront payment or private keys. Scammers may also impersonate trusted figures or companies. If you encounter such an offer, do not share any personal or financial details. Verify the source by checking official websites or trusted reviews. Report suspicious activity to relevant authorities and avoid clicking on unknown links. Staying cautious helps protect your assets and personal information from fraud.
