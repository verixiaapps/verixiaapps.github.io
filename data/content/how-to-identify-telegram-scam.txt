Telegram scams often appear as messages from unknown contacts offering prizes, investments, or urgent help requests. Common warning signs include requests for personal information, asking for money or cryptocurrency, unsolicited links, and poor grammar or spelling. Scammers may pretend to be friends, support staff, or official representatives. If you receive such a message, do not click on any links or share your details. Instead, verify the sender’s identity through other means and report the message to Telegram using the app’s built-in tools. Staying cautious and informed helps protect your privacy and finances from fraudulent schemes.
