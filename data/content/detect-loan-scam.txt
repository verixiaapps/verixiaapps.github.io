Loan scams often involve offers of easy money with little or no credit checks, usually through emails, calls, or ads. Scammers may ask for upfront fees, personal information, or pressure you to act quickly. Common warning signs include requests for payment before approval, unrealistic promises, and lack of official documentation. If you receive such an offer, do not share your personal details or send money. Instead, verify the lender’s legitimacy through official channels or government websites. Reporting the suspicious message to authorities helps protect others from falling victim. Always be cautious and take your time before committing to any loan offers.
