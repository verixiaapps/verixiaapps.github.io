Fake job offers often come as unsolicited emails or messages promising high pay with little effort. Scammers may ask for personal details, money upfront, or request payments for training or equipment. Common warning signs include vague job descriptions, lack of a company website, poor grammar, and unrealistic salary offers. Always verify the company by checking official websites and contacting them directly. Never share sensitive information or send money to unknown sources. If you suspect a scam, report it to local authorities or job fraud watchdogs and delete the message. Staying cautious helps protect your personal information and avoid financial loss.
