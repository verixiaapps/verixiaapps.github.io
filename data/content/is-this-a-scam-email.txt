Scam emails often pretend to be from trusted sources, like banks or popular companies, asking for personal information or urging quick action. Common warning signs include poor grammar, suspicious links, unknown senders, and unexpected requests for passwords or money. They may also create a sense of urgency to pressure you. If you receive a suspicious message, do not click on any links or provide information. Instead, verify the sender’s identity by contacting the company directly using official contact details. Report the email to your email provider or a relevant authority to help protect others. Staying cautious and informed is key to avoiding fraud.
