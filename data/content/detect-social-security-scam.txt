Social Security scams often involve calls or messages claiming there’s a problem with your account or benefits. Scammers may threaten arrest or demand immediate payment. Common warning signs include unsolicited contact, requests for personal information, threats, and payments via gift cards or wire transfers. Genuine agencies won’t ask for sensitive details this way or demand instant payment. If you receive such a message, do not respond or share personal data. Instead, hang up and contact the official Social Security office directly using the number on their website. Reporting the incident to authorities can help protect others from similar fraud. Stay cautious and verify all communications to avoid falling victim to these scams.
