Scam messages often pretend to be from trusted sources like banks or government agencies, asking for personal information or urgent action. They may contain spelling mistakes, unusual links, or requests for money. Common warning signs include threats, offers that seem too good to be true, and messages from unknown numbers or emails. If you receive such a message, do not click on any links or share personal details. Instead, verify the information independently by contacting the organization directly using official contact methods. Reporting the message to local authorities or the platform where you received it can help protect others. Staying cautious helps prevent falling victim to fraud.
