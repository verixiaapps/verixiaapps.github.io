Scam links often appear as urgent messages, fake promotions, or unexpected requests asking for personal information or money. They may look like official websites but have strange URLs or spelling errors. Common warning signs include unfamiliar senders, poor grammar, and pressure to act quickly. Suspicious links might also lead to downloads that harm your device. If you receive such a message, don’t click the link or provide any details. Instead, verify the source by contacting the company directly using official channels. Report the message to your email provider or relevant authorities to help prevent others from falling victim. Staying cautious helps protect your personal information and devices from harm.
