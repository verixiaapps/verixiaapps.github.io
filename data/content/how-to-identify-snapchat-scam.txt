Snapchat scams often appear as unexpected messages from strangers or even friends, asking for money, personal details, or to click suspicious links. Common warning signs include urgent requests, promises of free rewards, or messages with poor spelling and grammar. Scammers may also impersonate someone you know or create fake profiles. If you receive such a message, do not respond, avoid clicking any links, and never share personal information. Instead, block the sender and report the account to Snapchat through the app’s reporting feature. Staying cautious can help protect your privacy and security while using the platform.
