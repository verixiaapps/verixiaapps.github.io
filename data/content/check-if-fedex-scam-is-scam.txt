Scammers often pretend to be from FedEx, sending fake emails or messages about missed deliveries or fees. These scams usually ask for personal info or payment to release a package. Common warning signs include poor spelling, urgent language, suspicious links, or unexpected attachments. Legitimate FedEx messages rarely demand immediate payment or sensitive data via email. If you get a suspicious message, don’t click links or provide details. Instead, check your shipment status directly on the official FedEx website or contact their customer service. Staying cautious helps protect your personal information and avoids falling victim to fraud.
