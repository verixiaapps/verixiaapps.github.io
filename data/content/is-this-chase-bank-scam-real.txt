Scammers often impersonate big banks like Chase to trick people. These scams usually involve fake emails, texts, or calls claiming urgent issues with your account, asking for personal information or payment. Common warning signs include poor grammar, suspicious links, requests for sensitive data, and unsolicited contact. Legitimate banks never ask for passwords or secure info this way. If you receive a suspicious message or call, don’t click links or share details. Instead, contact your bank directly using official phone numbers or websites to verify. Reporting the attempt to your bank and relevant authorities can help protect others from falling victim. Stay cautious and verify before responding.
