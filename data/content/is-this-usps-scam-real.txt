Scammers often impersonate USPS to trick people into giving personal information or money. These messages may claim there’s a package problem, a fee to pay, or ask you to click on a suspicious link. Common warning signs include poor spelling, urgent demands, unknown senders, and requests for payment via gift cards or wire transfer. If you receive a suspicious message, do not click any links or provide personal details. Instead, verify by visiting the official USPS website or contacting their customer service directly. Reporting the scam to the Postal Inspection Service can also help protect others from falling victim. Always stay cautious with unexpected delivery alerts.
