Job scams often involve offers that seem too good to be true, like high pay for little work. Scammers may ask for personal information, upfront fees, or use poorly written messages. Common warning signs include requests for money, vague job details, pressure to act quickly, and unprofessional communication.

If you encounter a suspicious offer, do not share personal data or send money. Research the company independently and verify job listings on official websites. Report the incident to local authorities or job platforms to help protect others. Staying cautious helps you avoid falling victim to fraud and keeps your information safe.
