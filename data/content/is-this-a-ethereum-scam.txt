Scams involving Ethereum often promise quick profits or free coins in exchange for sending a small amount first. They usually come as messages, emails, or social media posts claiming to be from trusted sources or official accounts. Common warning signs include urgent requests for money, offers that sound too good to be true, and links asking for private keys or personal information. If you encounter this, do not send any funds or share sensitive details. Instead, verify the source through official channels and report the message to platform support. Always be cautious with unexpected offers related to cryptocurrency to protect your assets.
