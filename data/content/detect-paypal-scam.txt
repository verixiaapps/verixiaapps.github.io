PayPal scams often come as fake emails or messages claiming there’s a problem with your account or a suspicious transaction. These messages usually ask you to click a link, provide personal details, or send money. Common warning signs include poor spelling or grammar, urgent language, unfamiliar sender addresses, and links that don’t lead to the official website. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or share your information. Instead, log in directly to your account through the official site or app to check for issues. You can also forward suspicious emails to PayPal’s fraud team. Always stay cautious to protect your personal and financial information.
