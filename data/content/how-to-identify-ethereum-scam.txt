Scams involving Ethereum often appear as fake investment offers, giveaways, or urgent requests for private keys. Common warning signs include promises of guaranteed returns, messages from unknown sources, spelling mistakes, and requests for personal information or upfront payments. Scammers may also impersonate trusted figures or platforms. If you receive a suspicious message, do not share your private keys or send money. Verify the source through official channels and avoid clicking on unknown links. Reporting the incident to relevant platforms or authorities helps protect others. Stay cautious and always double-check before engaging with any unexpected offers related to your digital assets.
