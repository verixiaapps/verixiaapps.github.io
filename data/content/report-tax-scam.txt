Tax scams often involve fake calls, emails, or letters claiming you owe money or are entitled to a refund. Scammers may threaten arrest or demand immediate payment, usually via gift cards or wire transfers. Common warning signs include urgent language, requests for personal information, and poor spelling or grammar. If you receive such contact, do not provide any details or payment. Instead, verify the request by contacting the official tax agency directly using trusted contact information. You should also report the incident to the proper authorities to help protect others from similar fraud. Staying cautious and informed helps keep your personal information and money safe.
