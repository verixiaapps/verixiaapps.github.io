Scammers pretending to be from Wells Fargo often contact people by phone, email, or text, claiming there’s a problem with their account or suspicious activity. They may ask for personal information, passwords, or request immediate payment. Common warning signs include unexpected messages, requests for sensitive details, poor spelling or grammar, and urgent threats to close accounts. If you receive such contact, do not click links or share information. Instead, directly contact Wells Fargo using official phone numbers or the bank’s website to verify the message. Reporting the scam to Wells Fargo and the Federal Trade Commission helps protect others from fraud. Always stay cautious and verify before acting on any unexpected requests.
