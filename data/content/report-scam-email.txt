Scam emails often pretend to be from trusted sources like banks or popular companies, asking for personal information or urgent payments. They may include alarming messages, spelling mistakes, or suspicious links. Common warning signs include unknown senders, requests for sensitive data, and offers that seem too good to be true. If you receive one, do not reply or click any links. Instead, delete the message and report it to your email provider or relevant authorities. Reporting helps protect others and can assist in stopping these fraud attempts. Always stay cautious and verify unusual requests through official channels.
