Scam links often appear as urgent messages offering fake prizes, deals, or requests for personal information. They may look like official websites but contain strange URLs or spelling errors. Common warning signs include unexpected requests for money, poor grammar, unfamiliar senders, and suspicious attachments. If you receive a doubtful link, do not click it. Instead, verify the source by contacting the company directly using official contact details. Use online tools to check URLs or report the message to your email provider or relevant authorities. Staying cautious helps protect your personal information and devices from harm.
