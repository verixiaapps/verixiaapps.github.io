Scam messages often appear urgent or too good to be true, aiming to trick you into giving personal information or money. They may claim you’ve won a prize, need to verify accounts, or face legal trouble. Common warning signs include poor spelling, unfamiliar senders, suspicious links, and requests for sensitive data. If you receive such a message, avoid clicking any links or sharing details. Instead, delete it and report to the relevant authority or platform. Staying cautious and verifying messages helps protect your personal information and avoid fraud.
