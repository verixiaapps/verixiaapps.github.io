Phishing messages often pretend to be from trusted sources like banks or popular companies. They usually ask you to click a link, provide personal details, or download attachments. Common warning signs include poor spelling, urgent requests, unfamiliar sender addresses, and suspicious links. If you receive a questionable email, do not click any links or download attachments. Instead, verify the message by contacting the company directly using official contact information. Report the email as spam or phishing in your email client to help protect others. Staying cautious can prevent scams and keep your personal information safe.
