A phishing email is a fake message that tries to trick you into giving personal information, like passwords or credit card details. These scams often look like they come from trusted companies or banks and may ask you to click on suspicious links or download attachments. Common warning signs include poor spelling and grammar, urgent requests, unfamiliar sender addresses, and unexpected attachments. If you receive such an email, do not click on any links or share your information. Instead, verify the message by contacting the company directly using official contact details. Reporting the email to your email provider can also help protect others from falling victim to similar scams.
