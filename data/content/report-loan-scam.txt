Loan scams often involve fake offers promising easy money or low-interest rates. Scammers may ask for upfront fees, personal information, or payment before approving a loan. Common warning signs include requests for payment before approval, unsolicited calls or emails, pressure to act quickly, and deals that seem too good to be true. If you receive such an offer, do not share personal details or send money. Instead, verify the lender's legitimacy through official channels, report the suspicious activity to consumer protection agencies, and alert your bank. Staying cautious can help protect your finances and personal information from fraud.
