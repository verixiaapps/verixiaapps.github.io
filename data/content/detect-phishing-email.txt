Phishing emails are fake messages designed to steal your personal information or money. They often look like they come from trusted companies or people you know, asking you to click a link or provide sensitive details. Common warning signs include spelling mistakes, urgent requests, suspicious links, and unfamiliar sender addresses. The message may try to create a sense of panic, like threatening account closure. If you receive one, don’t click any links or download attachments. Instead, verify the request by contacting the company directly using official contact information. Report the message to your email provider or IT department to help protect others. Stay cautious and always double-check unexpected emails.
