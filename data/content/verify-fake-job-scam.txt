A fake job scam often involves offers that sound too good to be true, sometimes asking for personal information or upfront fees. Scammers may create emails or websites that look like real companies. Common warning signs include requests for money, vague job descriptions, unsolicited offers, poor grammar, and pressure to act quickly. If you receive such a suspicious offer, don’t share personal details or send money. Instead, verify the company’s contact information independently, check official job boards, and report the scam to authorities or consumer protection groups. Staying cautious helps protect your identity and finances from fraud.
