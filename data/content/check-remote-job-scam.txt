Remote job scams often promise easy work and high pay but ask for personal information or upfront fees. Scammers may pretend to be recruiters or companies and send fake offers via email or social media. Common warning signs include requests for money, vague job details, unsolicited contact, or pressure to act quickly. If you receive a suspicious offer, avoid sharing personal data or paying fees. Instead, research the company independently, verify the job posting on official sites, and report the message to authorities or the job platform. Staying cautious helps protect your identity and finances from fraud.
