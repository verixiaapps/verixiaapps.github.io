Tax scams often involve fake messages claiming you owe money or are due a refund, pressuring you to act quickly. Common signs include urgent threats, requests for personal details, or demands for payment via unusual methods like gift cards or wire transfers. Scammers may impersonate government officials and use official-sounding language. If you receive such a message, don’t respond or share personal information. Instead, verify its authenticity by contacting the official tax agency directly using trusted contact details from their official website. Reporting suspicious activity helps protect you and others from fraud. Staying cautious can prevent financial loss and identity theft.
