Scams involving Ethereum often promise unrealistic returns or ask for upfront payments to unlock funds. They may appear as messages from fake wallets, investment schemes, or impersonate well-known figures. Common warning signs include unsolicited requests for private keys, pressure to act quickly, poor grammar, and too-good-to-be-true offers. If you receive such a message, do not share your personal information or send any cryptocurrency. Verify the source independently and report suspicious activity to relevant platforms or authorities. Always stay cautious and research thoroughly before engaging with any crypto-related requests.
