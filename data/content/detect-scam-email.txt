Scam messages often appear urgent, with requests for personal information, money, or login details. They may mimic trusted companies, using fake logos and email addresses that look genuine. Common warning signs include spelling mistakes, unusual sender addresses, unexpected attachments, and suspicious links asking for sensitive data. If you receive a suspicious email, do not click any links or download attachments. Verify the message by contacting the company directly through official channels. Never share passwords or financial information. Reporting the message to your email provider or relevant authorities can help protect others. Staying cautious helps keep your information safe.
