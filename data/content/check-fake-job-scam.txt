Fake job offers often promise high pay for little work and ask for personal information or money upfront. Scammers may use official-sounding company names and send emails that look real but contain spelling errors or unusual email addresses. Common warning signs include requests for payment, vague job details, pressure to act fast, and communication only via text or messaging apps. If you receive a suspicious offer, do not share personal information or pay money. Verify the company by checking their official website or contacting them directly. Report the scam to job sites or consumer protection agencies to help others stay safe. Always trust your instincts and take time to research before accepting any job offer.
