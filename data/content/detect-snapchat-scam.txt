Scams on Snapchat often involve messages from fake profiles pretending to be friends or celebrities, asking for money, gifts, or personal information. Common warning signs include urgent requests, unfamiliar links, poor grammar, and offers that seem too good to be true. Scammers may also try to create pressure or fear to get a quick response. If you receive a suspicious message, avoid clicking on links or sharing any personal details. Block and report the sender to Snapchat immediately. Always verify messages through other trusted contacts before taking any action. Staying cautious helps protect your account and personal information from fraud.
