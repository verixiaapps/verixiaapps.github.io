Social Security scams often involve calls, texts, or emails claiming there’s a problem with your account or that you owe money. Scammers use urgent language to trick you into sharing personal information or sending payments. Common warning signs include unsolicited messages asking for sensitive details, threats of arrest, or requests to pay via gift cards or wire transfer. Real agencies will never demand immediate payment or threaten you. If you receive such a message, do not respond, click links, or share information. Instead, contact the official Social Security Administration directly using numbers from their website to verify the claim. Reporting suspicious contact helps protect you and others from fraud.
