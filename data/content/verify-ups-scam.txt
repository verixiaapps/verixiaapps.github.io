Scammers often send fake messages claiming to be from UPS, saying there’s a problem with a package delivery or requesting payment for customs fees. These messages may include suspicious links or ask for personal information. Common warning signs include poor grammar, urgent language, unfamiliar tracking numbers, and requests for payment via gift cards or wire transfers. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or provide personal details. Instead, visit the official UPS website directly or contact their customer service to verify the information. Always be cautious with unexpected messages about shipments to protect your personal and financial information.
