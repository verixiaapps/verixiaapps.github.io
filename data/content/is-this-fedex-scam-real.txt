Scams pretending to be from FedEx often involve fake delivery notifications or requests for payment to release a package. They usually come as emails, texts, or calls claiming there's a problem with a shipment. Common warning signs include unexpected messages, poor spelling or grammar, links asking for personal information, or urgent threats. Legitimate companies do not ask for sensitive details via these methods. If you receive such a message, do not click on any links or provide information. Instead, verify the shipment status directly through the official FedEx website or customer service. Reporting the suspicious contact to FedEx and your local authorities can help protect others from falling victim.
