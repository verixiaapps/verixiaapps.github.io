Fake job offers often promise high pay with little effort or urgent hiring. Scammers may ask for personal information, upfront fees, or payments for training or equipment. Common warning signs include poorly written messages, unrealistic job descriptions, requests for money, and contact through unofficial channels. They might claim to be from well-known companies but use suspicious email addresses. If you receive a suspicious offer, do not share personal details or send money. Instead, verify the company’s legitimacy through their official website or trusted sources. Report the incident to job platforms, consumer protection agencies, or local authorities to help prevent others from falling victim. Stay cautious and trust your instincts when a job seems too good to be true.
