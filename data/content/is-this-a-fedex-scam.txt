Scammers often impersonate delivery services, claiming a package is waiting or a fee is due. These messages may arrive as emails, texts, or calls, urging quick action to avoid delays. Watch for signs like poor spelling, suspicious links, unexpected charges, or requests for personal info. Legitimate companies rarely ask for payment via unusual methods or threaten immediate penalties. If you receive such a message, avoid clicking links or sharing sensitive details. Instead, contact the delivery service directly using official contact information from their website. Always verify your shipment status through trusted channels to stay safe from fraud.
