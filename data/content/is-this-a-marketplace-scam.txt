A common scam involves fake sellers or buyers on online marketplaces. Scammers often offer items at unusually low prices or request payment outside the platform. They might ask for personal information or rush you to complete a deal quickly. Warning signs include poor grammar, suspicious links, requests for wire transfers, and sellers with no reviews. If you encounter this, do not share personal details or make payments outside the official site. Report the user to the marketplace support team and block communication. Always use secure payment methods and trust your instincts—if something feels off, it’s better to walk away. Staying cautious helps protect your money and personal information.
