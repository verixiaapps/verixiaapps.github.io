Scam emails often pretend to be from trusted sources like banks or popular companies. They usually ask for personal information, urgent payments, or prompt you to click suspicious links. Common warning signs include poor grammar, unexpected requests, unfamiliar sender addresses, and threats of immediate action. If you receive such a message, do not reply or click any links. Instead, verify the information by contacting the company directly through official channels. It’s also helpful to report the email to your email provider or a relevant authority to protect yourself and others from fraud. Staying cautious can prevent potential loss or identity theft.
