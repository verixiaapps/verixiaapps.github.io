A common Bitcoin scam often promises quick profits or asks for a small payment to unlock a large reward. Scammers might impersonate trusted figures or companies to gain trust. Warning signs include unsolicited messages, requests for upfront payments, unrealistic promises, and poor spelling or grammar. If you receive such an offer, avoid clicking any links or sharing personal information. Research the source carefully and never send money to unknown individuals. Report suspicious activity to official platforms or consumer protection agencies to help prevent fraud. Staying cautious can protect your finances and personal data from being compromised.
