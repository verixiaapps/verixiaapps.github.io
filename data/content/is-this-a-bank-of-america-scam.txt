Scams pretending to be from Bank of America often come as urgent emails, texts, or calls claiming there’s a problem with your account. They may ask for personal details, passwords, or prompt you to click suspicious links. Common warning signs include poor spelling, generic greetings like “Dear Customer,” unexpected requests for information, and threats to close your account. Authentic messages usually address you by name and won’t ask for sensitive data via email or text. If you receive such communication, do not respond or click any links. Instead, contact Bank of America directly using the official website or phone number to verify the message’s legitimacy. Safeguard your information and report suspicious activity promptly.
