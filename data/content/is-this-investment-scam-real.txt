Some investment scams promise high returns with little risk and pressure you to act quickly. They often use flashy websites, unsolicited calls, or emails claiming you're a special opportunity. Warning signs include promises of guaranteed profits, requests for upfront payments, lack of clear information, and unlicensed sellers. If you receive such an offer, do not share personal details or send money. Instead, verify the company through official financial regulator websites and seek advice from trusted sources. Always take time to research and be cautious of deals that seem too good to be true. Staying informed helps protect your money and avoid fraud.
