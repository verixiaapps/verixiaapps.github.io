Loan scams often promise easy approval and quick money but require upfront fees or personal information. They may come via phone, email, or social media, using urgent language to pressure you. Common warning signs include requests for payment before approval, lack of official paperwork, unrealistic promises, and unverified lenders. If you receive such an offer, do not share personal details or send money. Instead, research the company thoroughly, check reviews, and contact your bank or a trusted financial advisor. Reporting suspicious activity to consumer protection agencies can help prevent others from falling victim. Staying cautious protects your finances and personal data from fraudsters.
