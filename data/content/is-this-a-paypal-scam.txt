A common PayPal scam involves fake emails or messages claiming account issues or unauthorized charges. These messages often urge you to click a link or provide personal information. Warning signs include poor spelling, urgent language, suspicious links, and requests for passwords or financial details. Real PayPal communications usually address you by name and never ask for sensitive info via email. If you receive such a message, avoid clicking links or sharing information. Instead, log into your account directly through the official website or app to check for notifications. Report the suspicious message to PayPal's fraud team to help protect yourself and others from potential fraud.
