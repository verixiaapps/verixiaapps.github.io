Scams on Facebook often appear as fake messages, friend requests, or posts offering prizes, urgent help, or too-good-to-be-true deals. Common warning signs include poor spelling, suspicious links, requests for money or personal information, and pressure to act quickly. If you encounter such content, do not click any links or share personal details. Instead, use Facebook’s reporting tools to flag the suspicious activity. You should also block the sender and inform trusted contacts if the scam involves your account. Staying cautious helps protect your information and the safety of others online.
