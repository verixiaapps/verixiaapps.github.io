Phishing emails often pretend to be from trusted sources like banks or popular companies, asking you to click a link or provide personal information. They may contain urgent messages about account problems or suspicious activity. Common warning signs include poor spelling or grammar, unknown sender addresses, suspicious links, and requests for sensitive details. If you get one, don’t click any links or download attachments. Instead, delete the message and report it to your email provider or IT department. Always verify suspicious emails by contacting the company directly using official contact information. Staying cautious helps protect your personal data and prevents scams from spreading.
