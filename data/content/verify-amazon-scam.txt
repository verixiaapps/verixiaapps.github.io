Scammers often impersonate Amazon, sending fake emails or messages claiming there’s a problem with your order or account. These messages may ask for personal details, payment info, or prompt you to click suspicious links. Common warning signs include poor spelling, urgent language, unfamiliar sender addresses, and requests for sensitive data. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or provide information. Instead, go directly to the official Amazon website or app to check your account. You can also report the message to Amazon’s official fraud team. Staying cautious helps protect your information and avoid falling victim to these fraud attempts.
