Many fraudsters pretend to be the IRS to trick people. These scams often involve calls, emails, or messages claiming you owe taxes and must pay immediately to avoid arrest or penalties. Common warning signs include aggressive threats, requests for payment via gift cards or wire transfers, and demands for personal information. The real IRS usually contacts by mail first and never demands immediate payment without giving you a chance to question it. If you receive such a message, don’t respond or share personal details. Instead, report it to the official IRS website and contact the IRS directly using verified phone numbers to confirm your tax status. Staying cautious helps protect you from fraud.
