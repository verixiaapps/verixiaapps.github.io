Social security scams often involve fake calls, emails, or messages claiming there is a problem with your benefits or account. Scammers may threaten legal action or ask for personal information like your Social Security number or bank details. Common warning signs include urgent requests for money, demands for payment through gift cards or wire transfers, and callers refusing to provide official contact information. If you receive such a suspicious message, do not share any personal details or send money. Instead, hang up and contact the official Social Security Administration directly using verified phone numbers or their official website to report the incident and confirm your account status. Staying cautious helps protect your identity and finances.
