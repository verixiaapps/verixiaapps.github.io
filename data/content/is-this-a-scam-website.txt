Sometimes, fake sites try to trick people into giving money or personal details. These often look very real, with logos and professional designs. Common warning signs include suspicious URLs, poor spelling, offers that seem too good to be true, and requests for sensitive information like passwords or credit card numbers. If you come across one, avoid clicking any links or downloading files. Do not share personal information. Instead, verify the site by searching for reviews or contacting the official company directly. Reporting the suspicious site to authorities or your browser can help protect others. Staying cautious helps keep your information safe online.
