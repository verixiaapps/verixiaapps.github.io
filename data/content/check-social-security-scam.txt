Scammers often impersonate government officials, claiming there is an issue with your social security number or benefits. They may ask for personal information or demand immediate payment to avoid legal trouble. Warning signs include unsolicited calls, threats of arrest, pressure to pay quickly, and requests for sensitive details like your Social Security number or bank info. Official agencies will never ask for payments by gift card or wire transfer. If you receive such a call or message, do not share any information. Instead, hang up and report the incident to the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) or the Social Security Administration’s fraud hotline. Stay cautious to protect your identity and finances.
