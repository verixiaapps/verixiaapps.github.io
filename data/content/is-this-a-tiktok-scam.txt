Many scams on TikTok involve fake giveaways, job offers, or messages asking for personal info or money. Scammers often create urgent posts promising prizes or easy cash to trick users. Common warning signs include requests for payment upfront, suspicious links, poor grammar, and profiles with few followers or recent creation dates. If you receive such messages, do not click links or share personal details. Verify the offer by checking official sources or contacting TikTok support. Reporting the suspicious account helps protect others. Always stay cautious and remember that genuine opportunities rarely require payment or private information.
