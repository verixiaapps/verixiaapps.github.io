Scams pretending to be from Bank of America often involve fake calls, emails, or texts claiming there’s a problem with your account. They may ask for personal details or money to “fix” the issue. Common warning signs include urgent threats, spelling mistakes, unusual sender addresses, or requests for sensitive information. Legitimate contacts won’t pressure you or ask for passwords. If you get a suspicious message, don’t click links or share details. Instead, contact Bank of America directly using official phone numbers or the website to verify. Reporting the message helps protect you and others from fraud. Staying cautious is key to avoiding these scams.
