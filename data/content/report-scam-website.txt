Scam websites often pretend to be legitimate online stores, banks, or service providers to steal personal information or money. They may offer deals that are too good to be true or ask for sensitive details like passwords and credit card numbers. Common warning signs include poor website design, misspelled words, suspicious URLs, and urgent requests for payment or information. If you come across a suspicious site or receive messages from one, avoid clicking links or giving any personal data. Instead, report it to consumer protection agencies or your internet service provider. Staying cautious helps protect you and others from fraud.
