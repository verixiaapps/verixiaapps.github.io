Scams on messaging apps like Telegram often involve fake messages claiming you’ve won prizes, need to verify your account, or must send money urgently. Common warning signs include unsolicited requests for personal information, urgent language, poor spelling, and suspicious links. Scammers may impersonate official accounts or contacts you know. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or share personal details. Instead, verify the sender’s identity through official channels and report the message to the app’s support team. Always be cautious with unexpected requests, and remember that legitimate organizations rarely ask for sensitive information through chat messages.
