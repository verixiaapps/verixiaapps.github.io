A romance scam often involves someone pretending to be interested in a relationship to gain trust and eventually ask for money or personal information. Common warning signs include quickly declaring strong feelings, refusing to meet in person, inconsistent stories, and requests for financial help. If you receive such messages, do not share personal details or send money. Instead, verify the person’s identity through video calls or social media, and report suspicious behavior to the dating platform or local authorities. Staying cautious helps protect your heart and your wallet from fraudsters.
