Scams pretending to be from Chase Bank often involve fake emails, texts, or calls claiming there's a problem with your account or unusual activity. They may ask for personal information, passwords, or payments. Common warning signs include poor grammar, urgent requests, suspicious links, or unfamiliar phone numbers. Official messages usually address you by name and never ask for sensitive details through unsecured channels. If you receive a suspicious message, do not click any links or provide information. Instead, contact Chase directly using the phone number on their official website or your bank statement to confirm if the communication is real. Reporting the scam helps protect you and others from fraud.
