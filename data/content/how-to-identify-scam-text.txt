Scam messages often pretend to be from trusted sources, like banks or popular companies, asking for personal information or money. They may include urgent language, spelling mistakes, or suspicious links. Common warning signs include unexpected requests, offers that seem too good to be true, and poor grammar. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or share your details. Instead, verify the information by contacting the organization directly using official contact methods. Reporting the message to your service provider or local authorities can help protect others from falling victim. Always stay cautious and trust your instincts when something feels off.
