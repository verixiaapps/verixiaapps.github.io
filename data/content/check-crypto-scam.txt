Crypto scams often appear as offers promising high returns, fake investment opportunities, or urgent requests to send money or personal info. Common warning signs include unsolicited messages, pressure to act quickly, fake websites or social media profiles, and promises that sound too good to be true. Scammers may also use official-sounding language to seem trustworthy. If you receive such a message, do not click on any links or share your details. Verify the source by researching independently and consult trusted resources or authorities. Reporting the suspicious communication to relevant platforms or law enforcement helps protect others from falling victim. Stay cautious and take your time before making any decisions involving digital currency.
