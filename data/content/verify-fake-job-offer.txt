Fake job offers often seem too good to be true, promising high pay for little work or quick hiring without interviews. Scammers may use official logos and ask for personal information or fees upfront. Common warning signs include requests for payment, poor grammar, vague job details, and email addresses that don’t match the company's domain. If you receive a suspicious offer, don’t share personal info or send money. Instead, verify by contacting the company directly using official contacts found on their website. Check online reviews or warning lists and report the offer to job boards or authorities to help protect others. Always stay cautious and research before responding.
