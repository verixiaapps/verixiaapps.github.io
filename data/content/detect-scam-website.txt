Scam sites often mimic real websites to trick visitors into sharing personal information or money. They may have poor design, spelling mistakes, or suspicious URLs that don’t match the official site. Common warning signs include urgent messages asking for immediate action, offers that seem too good to be true, and requests for sensitive data like passwords or credit card details. If you come across a suspicious site or link, avoid clicking on anything, don’t enter personal information, and close the page immediately. It’s important to report it to your web browser or relevant authorities and run a security scan on your device to stay safe.
