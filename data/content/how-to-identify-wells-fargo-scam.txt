Scammers pretending to be from Wells Fargo often send urgent messages asking for personal or financial information. They may claim there’s a problem with your account or offer fake prizes. Common warning signs include requests for passwords, Social Security numbers, or immediate payment via unusual methods like gift cards. Look out for poor spelling, suspicious email addresses, or unsolicited phone calls. If you get such a message, do not respond or click any links. Instead, contact Wells Fargo directly using official phone numbers or their website to verify the alert. Reporting the scam to the bank and local authorities helps protect others from falling victim. Always stay cautious and protect your personal information.
