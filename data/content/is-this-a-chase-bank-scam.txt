Scams involving Chase Bank often come as fake emails, texts, or calls claiming there’s an issue with your account. They may ask for personal info, passwords, or direct you to suspicious links. Common warning signs include unexpected messages asking for sensitive details, poor spelling or grammar, urgent threats, and unfamiliar phone numbers or email addresses. Legitimate messages usually come from official Chase contacts and never ask for your password. If you receive such a message, do not click any links or provide information. Instead, contact Chase directly using the phone number on their official website to verify. Reporting suspicious activity helps protect you and others from fraud.
