Frequently asked questions

What is a DEX aggregator and why does it get better prices?
A DEX aggregator routes your swap across multiple decentralized exchanges simultaneously rather than executing on a single pool. On Solana, liquidity is fragmented across Raydium, Orca, Meteora, and dozens of other venues. A direct swap on any one DEX applies full price impact to that pool. An aggregator finds the deepest available pool or splits your order across pools to reduce impact -- the result is more tokens received for the same input amount.
What perpetuals trading is available?
Perpetual futures are available via Hyperliquid -- the leading on-chain perps exchange with a fully on-chain orderbook on its own L1. Trade BTC, ETH, SOL and 500+ markets with up to 50x leverage. No KYC, non-custodial, $1B+ daily volume.
Is the swap non-custodial? Do I need to sign up?
Yes -- non-custodial means your private keys and funds stay in your wallet until the swap executes on-chain. There is no account, no sign-up, and no KYC at any point. Connect your wallet, review the quoted price, and confirm the transaction. Your tokens move directly through the smart contract.
What is slippage tolerance and how should I set it?
Slippage tolerance is the maximum price deviation you will accept between the quoted rate and the actual fill price. Setting 1% means you accept any fill up to 1% worse than quoted. On liquid pairs, 0.5% is usually sufficient. On thinner pools or new tokens, you may need 1-2%.
What is the difference between swapping on Raydium directly versus using a DEX aggregator?
A direct Raydium swap executes at the current CLMM pool price with full price impact applied to your order size. An aggregator runs the same trade simultaneously across Raydium, Orca, Meteora, and other active pools, then either routes the full order through the deepest pool or splits it to reduce impact. The difference in execution cost is measurable on trades above $5,000 and significant on trades above $25,000.
