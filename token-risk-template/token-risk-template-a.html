How to Check If a Crypto Token Is Safe

Contract structure Unrestricted minting, owner withdrawal functions, and blacklisting capabilities can allow developers to drain value or block selling.
Liquidity depth Thin or unlocked liquidity pools make it easy for creators to remove all liquidity, collapsing the price instantly and permanently.
Holder distribution When a handful of wallets hold most of the supply, they can exit simultaneously and push price down faster than the market can absorb.
Honeypot patterns Honeypot tokens allow purchases but block selling at the contract level. They can look like any other token until you try to exit.

What the token risk checker analyzes

The checker reads the token smart contract directly to identify structural risk signals before they appear in the price. It looks for unrestricted minting -- the ability to create new supply at any time -- which can dilute existing holders without warning. It checks freeze authority, which allows developers to pause all token transfers, and blacklisting functions that can prevent specific wallets from selling. These functions are written into the contract at deployment and are visible on-chain long before any price impact occurs.

Liquidity pool structure matters as much as the contract itself. A thin pool means large exits move the price disproportionately. An unlocked pool means developers can remove all liquidity in a single transaction, collapsing the price to near zero in the same block. According to FBI and FTC reports, crypto-related investment losses exceeded $5.6B in 2023 -- the majority involving tokens where pool structure gave clear advance warning. The checker surfaces pool depth alongside lock status so you can see both risk factors together.

Holder concentration shows how coordinated selling risk is distributed. When the top five wallets hold the majority of supply, a coordinated exit -- or even one large holder deciding to leave -- can move the market faster than other buyers can absorb. The checker shows top wallet percentages, pool depth, and contract permissions together so you have a complete structural picture before committing to a position.

What is a honeypot token?
A honeypot token is a smart contract that allows purchases but blocks selling. The restriction is encoded into the transfer function -- a require() statement that causes sell transactions to revert for non-whitelisted wallets. Honeypots look identical to legitimate tokens on price charts because buy transactions execute normally. Only sell attempts fail, and they fail silently at gas cost with no receipt.
What is a rug pull and how does the checker detect it?
A rug pull happens when developers remove all liquidity from the trading pool, collapsing the price to near zero in a single transaction. The checker detects it by checking whether LP tokens are locked -- if they are not, the pool can be drained instantly. It also surfaces pool depth relative to market cap: a $180,000 pool supporting a $2.4M market cap means a single $15,000 exit moves price 8% before slippage activates.
